# 46. SWAN: A Study of Solar Wind Anisotropies on SOHO with Lyman Alpha Sky Mapping / SOHO의 Lyman α 전천 매핑을 통한 태양풍 이방성 연구

---

## 1. Core Contribution / 핵심 기여

**한국어 / Korean**
이 논문은 1995년 12월 발사 예정이었던 SOHO 우주선의 SWAN(Solar Wind ANisotropies) 기기에 대한 결정적인 사전 발사 종합 논문이다. SWAN의 과학적 사명은 "원격 탐사로 태양풍의 헬리오그래픽 위도 의존성을 측정하는 것"이며, 이를 위해 행성간 수소 원자가 태양 Lyman α(121.6 nm) 광자를 공명 산란하여 만드는 전 천구의 휘도 분포를 1° 해상도로 매핑한다. 핵심 물리 메커니즘은 태양풍 양성자(H⁺)와 성간 중성 수소(H) 사이의 전하교환 반응으로, 이 반응이 태양 주변에 비대칭적인 "이온화 공동(ionization cavity)"을 만들고 그 모양은 태양풍 질량 플럭스의 위도 분포를 직접 반영한다. Prognoz 5/6(1976–77)의 선행 분석이 25–30 % 극지방 플럭스 결손을 시사했고, 1994년 Ulysses의 직접 관측이 이를 in-situ로 확인했다. SWAN은 이 두 결과를 잇는 "연속 모니터링" 도구로 설계되었다.

**English / 영어**
This paper is the definitive pre-launch overview of the SWAN (Solar Wind ANisotropies) instrument flown on SOHO (launched December 1995). SWAN's primary mission is to measure the heliographic-latitude dependence of the solar wind by remote sensing, accomplished by mapping the full sky at 1° resolution in the 121.6 nm Lyman α emission produced by resonant scattering of solar photons on inflowing interstellar neutral hydrogen. The driving physics is charge exchange between solar wind protons (H⁺) and interstellar neutrals (H): this reaction carves an asymmetric "ionization cavity" around the Sun whose shape directly encodes the latitudinal solar wind mass flux. Earlier Prognoz 5/6 analyses (1976–77) had hinted at a 25–30 % polar mass-flux deficit, and Ulysses confirmed this in-situ in 1994; SWAN was designed as the missing continuous-monitoring tool linking those two milestone results. The paper presents the methodology, forward modeling, hardware (twin sensor units, two-mirror periscope, hydrogen absorption cell, multi-anode MCP detector), and ancillary science targets (corona, geocorona, comets, heliopause, solar Lyα variations).

## 2. Reading Notes / 읽기 노트

### Part I: Solar Wind Imprint on the Interstellar H Flow / 성간 H 흐름에 미치는 태양풍의 흔적 (Section 2, pp. 405–411)

**한국어 / Korean**
저자들은 먼저 태양풍 질량 플럭스 측정의 중요성을 강조한다. 고전적 Parker 열적 모델은 약 250 km/s의 종단속도까지는 설명할 수 있지만 400 km/s 이상의 고속풍은 설명하지 못한다. 코로나홀에서 나오는 빠른 태양풍은 비-열적 에너지 플럭스의 존재를 요구하며, 그 진폭은 극 질량 플럭스 값에 강하게 의존한다(Lallement, Holzer, Munro 1986). 따라서 극의 질량 플럭스 정확값은 태양풍 가속 모델의 결정적 입력이다.

방법론(Section 2.2)의 핵심 아이디어: 성간 매질에서 흘러들어오는 중성 수소는 태양풍에 의해 전하교환 이온화되어 사라진다. 성간 광자(EUV)에 의한 이온화도 있지만 이것은 거의 등방적이다. 만약 태양풍이 등방적이라면 성간 H의 분포는 태양–성간풍 축에 대해 축대칭일 것이고 Lyα 휘도 패턴도 축대칭일 것이다. 그러나 실제 관측은 축비대칭을 보이며, 이 비축대칭이 바로 태양풍 위도 의존성의 흔적이다.

특징적 공간 스케일:
- 상풍(upwind) 방향의 방출 영역 크기: ~5 AU
- 하풍(downwind) 방향: ~20 AU
- 등방 모델로부터의 편차가 최대인 영역: ~10 AU
- 최대 편차는 하풍 방향에서 45° 떨어진 위치(극과 풍 축을 포함하는 평면)
- 성간풍 흐름 속도: 약 4 AU/year
- 따라서 하풍 영역은 3–4년 전의 태양풍을 반영, 상풍은 ~1.5년 전.

**English / 영어**
The authors begin by stressing the importance of solar wind mass-flux measurements. Parker's classical thermal model explains terminal speeds up to ~250 km/s but fails for the >400 km/s fast wind from coronal holes, which requires non-thermal energy flux whose amplitude depends strongly on the polar mass-flux value (Lallement, Holzer, Munro 1986). Hence the polar mass flux is a crucial input for solar-wind acceleration models.

Methodology (Section 2.2): the inflowing interstellar neutral H is ionized by charge exchange with solar wind protons; photoionization by solar EUV is also present but nearly isotropic. If both the solar wind and the EUV flux were isotropic, the H distribution would be axisymmetric about the Sun–wind axis. The observed departures from axisymmetry directly reflect latitudinal solar-wind variations. Note: photoionization is illuminated by the whole solar disk, hence largely latitude-independent, while charge-exchange is directional (H atoms at latitude δ are ionized by SW issued from latitude δ).

Characteristic spatial scales:
- Upwind emission region: ~5 AU
- Downwind: ~20 AU
- Region where departures from isotropic SW are maximal: ~10 AU
- Maximum effect at 45° from the downwind axis, in the plane containing the pole and the wind axis
- Interstellar inflow speed: ~4 AU/year
- Hence downwind reflects 3–4 yr of past SW, upwind ~1.5 yr.

After subtracting photoionization, the latitudinal variation of pure solar-wind ionization is obtained. Because charge-exchange cross-section depends on relative velocity (Fite et al. 1962), an independent estimate of the latitudinal velocity profile (e.g., from IPS measurements, Kojima & Kakinuma 1987) is needed to convert to mass flux. The cross-section drops by ~32 % from 350 to 750 km/s, so only the *remaining* part of the ionization decrease reflects the mass-flux variation.

Three remarks made by the authors are worth noting:
1. The solar-wind anisotropy idea applied to Lyα was first published by Joselyn & Holzer (1975), but they assumed *higher* polar wind — opposite to what was later found.
2. Newly created neutral H from charge exchange escapes at ~400 km/s and is Doppler-shifted out of the absorbing solar Lyα line — so it does not contribute to scattering.
3. The interstellar wind blows close to the ecliptic plane (β_w ≈ +7.5°). If it flowed near the pole, latitudinal SW variations would be nearly impossible to detect — a "lucky geometry."

**Previous results (Section 2.3):** Mariner 10, Prognoz 5/6, Voyager, Pioneer-Venus all showed 20–40 % decrease of total ionization toward the poles. Photoionization being nearly isotropic, the *pure-SW* decrease is 25–50 %. Using Kojima & Kakinuma's (1987) IPS velocity profile, the harmonic model gave 25–30 % polar mass-flux deficit in 1976–77, but only 0–10 % in 1985–86 — possibly due to the inclination of the magnetic axis at solar minimum.

### Part II: Forward Modeling and Inversion / 순방향 모델링과 역산 (Section 3, pp. 411–419)

**한국어 / Korean**
직접 역산은 불가능하다(Section 3.1). Lyα 강도는 시선 방향의 방출률을 적분한 것이고, 방출률 자체는 H 밀도와 태양 광자 플럭스의 곱이다. 또한 1 AU 관측 위치에 따라 시차(parallax) 효과로 최대 강도 방향이 20–40° 이동한다.

**조화 함수 모델(Harmonic model, 식 1)**: 최초의 분석에 사용된 단순한 모델로, β(λ,r) = β_e (1 − A sin²λ) r_e²/r². 이 형태는 H 원자의 궤적을 따라 해석적으로 적분 가능하다는 큰 장점이 있다. Prognoz 자료 적합 결과 A ≈ 0.40을 얻었다(그림 6).

**Summanen et al. (1993) 모델**: 위도 10° 너비의 슬라이스로 이온화율을 정의하여 Prognoz 자료를 재분석. 결과는 ±0°에서 ±30°까지 감소하다가 ±30°에서 ±70°까지 큰 평탄대(plateau)를 보인다. 결과(그림 7): 적도 부근의 좁은 고플럭스 영역과 ±30° 이상의 넓은 평탄대.

**Ulysses 직접 비교(그림 8)**: SWOOPS in-situ 측정은 같은 정성적 형태 — 적도 근처 변동(곡선 자기적도판) → ±15° 이상에서 약 2.0×10⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹의 거의 일정한 값으로 감소(평균 황도값 3×10⁸의 35–50 % 감소). Prognoz 결과보다 평탄대가 좁고 강도가 1.2–1.3배 높지만 전반적 형태는 매우 유사.

**복사 전달(Section 3.3)**: 광학적 두께 τ < 0.1/AU지만 상풍/하풍 비대칭 때문에 다중 산란 효과가 의외로 크다. Quémerais & Bertaux (1993)의 Monte Carlo 및 반복법 코드는 1.03–1.30 배의 강도 보정을 제시(상풍: 1.03, 하풍: 1.30).

**짧은 시간 스케일(Section 3.4)**: 상풍 MER(~2.5 AU)에서 보면 태양풍 변화에 가장 빠르게 반응. 식 (5): r_max = r_c/2, 식 (6): T(r_max) = (1/4) T_e²/T_D. T_e ≈ 87일(v=20 km/s 통과 시간)이므로 T_D = 1.5×10⁶ s ≈ 17일이면 T(r_max) ≈ 107일.

흥미로운 결과: 반응 시간이 H 원자 수명에 *반비례*. 이온화율이 크면(수명이 짧으면) H 원자가 태양에 더 가까이 접근하므로 빠른 변화에 잘 반응한다. 1.3년 주기의 태양풍 진동(Richardson et al. 1994)이 SWAN으로 검출 가능할 것으로 기대.

**한국어 / Korean** (계속)
**태양 Lyman α 이방성(Section 3.5)**: 발사 시점이 태양 극소이므로 큰 비대칭은 예상되지 않지만, 1083 nm Helium 라인 폭과 Lyα 라인 폭의 상관관계(Ajello et al. 1994)를 SUMER로 검증할 수 있다.

**태양 활동주기 변조(Section 3.6)**: H 원자 궤적은 중력+복사 압력으로 결정되는데, 두 힘이 거의 균형. 하지만 활동주기에 따라 순힘이 인력↔척력으로 변하면서 H 원자 궤적이 굴절·집속(focusing) 되어 밀도 분포에 시간 종속성을 만든다. 종단 충격파(termination shock) 위치도 변할 수 있다.

**English / 영어**
Direct inversion is impossible (Section 3.1). The Lyα intensity is a line-of-sight integral of the emissivity, which is the product of H density × solar photon flux. Moreover, at 1 AU the position-dependent parallax shifts the maximum-emission direction by 20–40° between observations spaced 6 months.

**Harmonic model (Eq. 1)**: β(λ,r) = β_e (1 − A sin²λ) r_e²/r², with A the anisotropy factor. The big advantage is that the line-of-sight integral can be done analytically along H trajectories. Fits to Prognoz data give A ≈ 0.40 (Figure 6, where the A=0 isotropic curve fits poorly).

**Summanen et al. (1993)**: replaced the harmonic model with 10°-wide latitude slices, reanalyzed the Prognoz data, and found a better fit with the ionization rate decreasing from 0° to ±30° and then a large plateau from ±30° to ±70°. The mass flux derived from this (using Zhao & Hundhausen 1983 velocity profile and Fite et al. 1962 cross-sections) is shown in Figure 7: narrow high-flux equatorial peak + broad plateau at ±30° to ±70° that is 35–50 % lower than the ecliptic mean.

**Ulysses comparison (Figure 8)**: SWOOPS in-situ measured n_p v_p R² as a function of heliographic latitude during the descent to the south solar pole (1992–94). The plot shows strong variations at λ < 15° (the curved heliospheric current sheet sweeping past) with peaks of 3.0–4.0×10⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹, then a nearly constant value of ~2.0×10⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹ above 15° — a 35 % deficit relative to the dashed line at 3×10⁸ representing the ecliptic mean. The Prognoz plateaus look broader and ~1.2–1.3× higher than Ulysses, but the general shape is strikingly similar.

**Radiative transfer (Section 3.3)**: Although τ < 0.1/AU, multiple scattering matters because of the strong upwind/downwind density asymmetry. Quémerais & Bertaux (1993) developed two codes (Monte Carlo + iterative); both give a multiplicative correction factor q ranging from 1.03 (upwind line of sight) to 1.30 (downwind). Going beyond axisymmetric H distributions to include latitudinal ionization variations requires a full 3-D radiative transfer or a semi-empirical approach.

**Short-timescale variations (Section 3.4)**: The MER at ~2.5 AU upwind reacts fastest to SW changes. Along the upwind axis the H density is

$$n(r) = n_\infty\, e^{-r_c/r}, \quad r_c = \beta_o r_e^2/v$$

The emissivity ε(r) ∝ n(r)/r² peaks at r_max = r_c/2, where the residence time is T(r_max) = (1/4) T_e²/T_D. With T_e = 87 days for v = 20 km/s, a "typical" T_D = 1.5×10⁶ s ≈ 17 days gives T(r_max) ≈ 107 days. Surprisingly, **the reaction time is *inversely* proportional to H lifetime**: larger ionization rates push H atoms closer to the Sun where they react faster. SWAN should be able to detect the 1.3-year solar-wind oscillation reported by Richardson et al. (1994).

A 1977 Prognoz observation found a 10 % dip in upwind intensity over a ~30° latitude width near the ecliptic — interpreted as a latitude-localized ionization peak from the (then very flat) heliospheric current sheet sweeping past the MER. This hints at the diagnostic power of MER-pointed observations.

**Solar Lyα anisotropy (Section 3.5)**: Launch at solar minimum predicts small departures from spherical symmetry, but longitudinal bulges from active regions will rotate with 26-day periodicity. SUMER will provide the line profile vs. latitude, important because the 20 km/s LIC inflow Doppler-shifts the excitation by ±0.08 Å.

**Solar-cycle modulation (Section 3.6)**: H atom trajectories are governed by a central force that is the sum of gravity and radiation pressure — nearly balanced for H. But during the solar cycle the net force oscillates between attractive and repulsive, refracting and focusing H trajectories. Time-dependent latitudinal changes and termination-shock motion are also potential modeling targets.

### Part III: SWAN Instrument Description / SWAN 기기 설명 (Section 4, pp. 419–433)

**한국어 / Korean**
**개요(Section 4.1)**: SWAN은 두 개의 동일한 센서 유닛(SU)으로 구성된다. 하나는 SOHO의 +Z(북황극), 다른 하나는 −Z(남황극) 면에 장착되어 각각 한 반구를 매핑. 각 SU의 순간 시야는 5×5° (25 화소 × 1×1°), 페리스코프 메커니즘으로 ±130° 회전 가능. 1° 해상도로 전 천구 매핑을 1일 미만에 완료. 한 화소당 적분 시간은 보통 45초.

기기 사양(Table I):
- 총 질량: 13.25 kg
- 평균 전력: 11 W
- 텔레메트리: 200 bps
- 파장 범위: 115–180 nm
- 광도 감도: 1° 화소당 1 Rayleigh 당 0.75 counts/s
- 검출기: Solar blind MgF₂/CsI 광음극, 다중 양극 MCP
- 카운트 율: 200/s (행성간) ~ 10⁴/s (코로나)
- 상대 정확도: IP 신호 45초 카운팅 시 1 % 미만
- 수소 셀 분광 분해능: 0.01 Å 미만 (분해능 10⁵)

**광학 시스템(Section 4.3)**: 페리스코프는 두 개의 45° 입사각 토로이드 거울로 구성되어 직교 회전축으로 ±130° 범위에서 전 반구를 가리킨다. 1° 미만의 정확도, 90 폴 Sagem 스테퍼 모터로 마이크로스텝 구동. 거울 코팅은 Al + MgF₂ (Lyα에서 ~77 % 반사율). H 셀은 MgF₂ 렌즈 두 개와 H₂ 가스로 채워진 파이렉스 용기로, 텅스텐 필라멘트로 H₂를 가열 분해해 원자 H 흡수띠(중심 ±20 mÅ ≈ 5 km/s 도플러)를 생성.

**검출기(Section 4.3.4)**: Hamamatsu MCP, 25개 1°×1° 화소 + 2개 다크 카운트용 측면 화소. 검출 효율 ρ_c ≈ 0.5, 양자 효율 Q ≈ 8–10 %. 한 SU의 광도 식: N_p(1°,1R) = 0.75 cps. 일반적 500 R 강도에서 50 % 효율로 374 cps → 45초 적분 시 17×10³ counts → 통계 정확도 0.8 %.

**수소 흡수 셀(Section 4.3.5)**: 1976 Bertaux 등의 OGO-5 실험에서 처음 사용. 셀 ON일 때 흡수 함수 R(θ,φ) = I_ON/I_OFF. SOHO-우주선과 성간풍의 상대속도 V_r은 10–50 km/s로 변하므로 도플러 시프트가 5 km/s 이상이면 흡수가 0(R=1)이 된다. R_min에서 선폭 → 가스 온도 추정. DASS(Doppler Angular Spectral Scanning, Bertaux & Lallement 1984)는 다른 시선의 R 측정으로 행성간 Lyα 라인 프로파일을 deconvolution으로 재구성.

**English / 영어**
**Overview (Section 4.1)**: SWAN consists of two identical Sensor Units. One is mounted on SOHO's +Z (near the north ecliptic pole) face, the other on −Z (near the south). Each SU has an instantaneous FOV of 5×5° divided into 25 pixels of 1×1°, steered by a two-mirror periscope to point anywhere in its hemisphere. A full-sky 1° map can be acquired in less than a day. Integration time per pointing: ~45 s.

Specifications (Table I): total mass 13.25 kg, average power 11 W, telemetry 200 bps, wavelength range 115–180 nm, photometric sensitivity 0.75 counts/s per Rayleigh per 1° pixel, expected count rates 200/s (interplanetary) to 10⁴/s (corona), better than 1 % relative accuracy on the IP signal.

**Optical system (Section 4.3)**: A 2-mirror torical periscope provides ±130° rotation about each of two orthogonal axes — full hemispheric coverage. Mechanism accuracy 0.1°, driven by a 90-pole Sagem stepper motor at up to 0.4 Nm. Mirror coating Al + MgF₂ → 77 % reflectivity at Lyα. The H cell is a Pyrex vessel between two MgF₂ lenses, filled with H₂ at a few hundred Pa; tungsten filaments heat-dissociate H₂ → 2H, producing an atomic H absorption notch (FWHM ≈ ±20 mÅ ≈ 5 km/s Doppler).

**Detector (Section 4.3.4)**: Hamamatsu multi-anode MCP, 25 active 1°×1° pixels + 2 side pixels for dark current; one side pixel has a BaF₂ window blocking λ < 140 nm. Photocathode CsI on MgF₂ → "solar blind" cut-off at 200 nm. Counting efficiency ρ_c ≈ 0.5, quantum efficiency Q ≈ 8–10 %. The photometric equation:

$$N_p(1°) = I \cdot S \cdot \Omega \cdot T_m^2 \cdot T_c \cdot \rho_c \cdot Q$$

with S = 5.72 cm² (entrance pupil), Ω = 3×10⁻⁴ sr (1° pixel), T_m² = 0.60 (mirrors), T_c = 0.20 (cell), ρ_c = 0.5, Q = 0.09 → 0.75 counts/s per 1°/Rayleigh. For a typical 500 R sky region, 374 counts/s × 45 s = 17 000 counts, giving 0.8 % statistical accuracy on intensity and 1.1 % on the cell-on/cell-off reduction factor.

**Hydrogen cell (Section 4.3.5)**: Pioneered by Bertaux et al. on OGO-5 (1977). When the filament is ON, the cell absorbs photons within ±20 mÅ of line center; when OFF, the gas recombines (within 0.05 s) and is fully transparent to Lyα. The reduction factor R(θ,φ) = I_ON/I_OFF is the central observable. Because SOHO orbits the L1 point at ~30 km/s and the LISM flow is 20 km/s, the relative velocity V_r varies from 10 to 50 km/s through the year; for V_r > 30 km/s the absorption is negligible (R = 1) — defining a "Zero Doppler Shift Circle" perpendicular to V_r where R is minimum (Bertaux & Lallement 1984). The DASS technique uses R measurements over many directions to deconvolve the IP Lyα line profile.

Table III lists four operating regimes of cell SW-12 (filament 1/2, low/high voltage), with equivalent absorption widths from 20 to 38 mÅ. Filament lifetime ~2000 hours at 50 % duty cycle.

**Spectral efficiency (Section 4.3.3)**: MgF₂ lenses + CsI cathode give cut-offs at 115 nm and 200 nm. A 10-nm Lyα interference filter was considered but rejected — would have eliminated 99 % of stellar contamination but at a 6× sensitivity loss; only 173 of 41 242 sky pixels exceed 10 cps from stars without filter.

**Chromatic aberration**: The MgF₂ refractive index varies rapidly across the FUV. At ~120 nm the 1° beam is fully contained in one pixel; at other wavelengths adjacent pixels are also illuminated (relevant for star/galactic-background contamination).

**Hydrogen cell calibration (Figs. 15, 18)**: Ground tests at the 10-m VUV spectrometer at Observatoire de Meudon measured the cell's transmission profile (FWHM 39 mÅ for SW-11) and confirmed the model line profile. A pure Gaussian absorption convolved with the spectrometer instrumental profile reproduces the measurements.

**English / 영어**
**Heliopause (Section 5)**: Charge exchange at the heliopause (a) decelerates H from 26 km/s (LIC, He value) to 20 km/s (measured for H), (b) reduces H density inside the heliosphere (filtration), (c) distorts the velocity distribution from Maxwellian. Some Prognoz cell observations could not be fit by simple "hot" models, hinting at heliopause perturbations. Several lines of evidence (Gurnett et al. 1993, Cummings, Stone & Webber 1993, Quémerais et al. 1995) suggest a relatively close heliopause. The SWAN reduction-factor maps will compare to Baranov–Malama H-distribution theory and constrain the heliopause distance.

**Corona (Section 6)**: Coronal Lyα drops from 7 kR at 4 R_S to 1 kR at 5 R_S (Withbroe et al. 1982). Above 5 R_S the IP background is comparable. SWAN's sun shade allows observation down to 10 R_S, providing the IP-background subtraction for UVCS at high altitudes (R > 5 R_S). With the H cell, SWAN can measure coronal Lyα linewidth (averaged over 1°). CME tracking and possible recombination-Lyα emission are bonus targets.

**Comets (Section 7)**: Lyα is the brightest UV emission in comets — H atoms photodissociated from H₂O surround the nucleus over 2×10⁷ km (Bennett comet, Bertaux et al. 1973). SWAN can monitor ~12 comets/yr. Crucially, comets within 45° of the Sun cannot be observed from ground or HST/IUE — SWAN's exclusive territory. The H production rate Q(H) is derived from N(p) = Q/(4vp) where v is the expansion velocity (typically 8 km/s from H₂O photodissociation) and p the impact parameter. Pre-discovery detection: Bradfield 2 would have been 100 R bright on March 30, 1992, vs. ground discovery May 31 (Figure 19).

**Geocorona (Section 8)**: Earth's exosphere extends >10⁵ km. Lyα brightness 25 kR on the disc, 100 R at 10⁵ km. From SOHO at 1.5×10⁶ km the geocoronal "spot" is ~15° across at 24° from anti-solar direction. Daily 1° SWAN maps will monitor the global geocorona vs. solar activity, comparing to OGO-5 (1968–69) which found nonthermal H escape ~10 % above Jeans rate. The geotail (Thomas & Bohlin 1972) is expected trailing in the Earth's orbital motion direction.

**Solar Lyα (Section 9)**: SUMER will provide high-resolution solar Lyα profiles, essential because the 20 km/s LIC inflow excites H atoms in different parts of the solar profile depending on position (range ±0.08 Å). The interplanetary H itself acts as a giant resonance cell — analogous to Grec et al.'s (1980) Na D experiment that discovered the 5-minute solar oscillations. Pointing the two SUs at the two ecliptic poles gives independent global hemispheric responses.

## 3. Key Takeaways / 핵심 시사점

1. **Charge-exchange ionization is the directional probe of solar wind latitude / 전하교환 이온화는 태양풍 위도의 방향성 탐침이다** — Whereas photoionization by EUV is essentially isotropic (full-disk illumination), charge exchange depends on the local SW proton density and velocity at the latitude of each H atom. The departure of the IP Lyα map from axisymmetry is therefore a *direct* signature of latitudinal SW variations. / 광이온화가 등방인 반면 전하교환은 H 원자의 위도에서의 양성자 밀도·속도에 직접 의존하므로, Lyα 지도의 비축대칭성이 곧 태양풍 위도 변화의 직접적 흔적이다.

2. **The Maximum Emissivity Region (MER) at ~2.5 AU upwind is the "fast clock" / 상풍 ~2.5 AU의 최대 방출 영역(MER)이 빠른 시계 역할을 한다** — Eq. (5)–(6) show the reaction time to ionization changes is T(r_max) = (1/4) T_e²/T_D ≈ 80–160 days. Importantly, this time *decreases* with increasing ionization rate, allowing MER pointings to detect the 1.3-year SW oscillation (Richardson et al. 1994). / r_max=r_c/2와 T(r_max)=T_e²/(4T_D) 식으로부터 반응시간이 80–160일이며, 이온화율이 높아질수록 *짧아진다*는 역설적 결과로 1.3년 주기 진동까지 검출 가능.

3. **The harmonic anisotropy model has analytical line-of-sight integrals / 조화 이방성 모델은 시선 방향 적분이 해석적이다** — β(λ,r) = β_e(1 − A sin²λ) r⁻² admits a closed-form integral along H trajectories, making forward modeling computationally feasible in 1976–95 hardware. Prognoz fits gave A ≈ 0.40, well above the isotropic A = 0 model. / sin²λ 형태의 단순함 덕분에 H 궤적 적분이 닫힌 형태로 가능하여 Prognoz 분석을 가능하게 했고, A ≈ 0.40은 등방 모델 A=0과 명확히 구별된다.

4. **Charge-exchange cross-section velocity dependence couples ionization rate to mass flux non-trivially / 전하교환 단면적의 속도 의존성이 이온화율과 질량 플럭스를 비단순 결합한다** — σ(v_rel) decreases by ~32 % from 350 to 750 km/s. Therefore a measured ionization-rate decrease of, say, 40 % at the pole partly reflects the higher v_p (smaller σ) and only partly the lower n_p. An external velocity profile (e.g. IPS) is required to disentangle. / σ가 v_rel에 따라 32 % 감소하므로 이온화율 감소가 곧 질량 플럭스 감소와 같지 않다. IPS 같은 독립적 속도 분포가 있어야 분리 가능.

5. **Two-sensor periscope design with 1° resolution achieves full-sky coverage in <1 day / 두 센서·페리스코프 설계로 1° 해상도 전천 매핑을 1일 이내에 달성** — Each 5×5° FOV with 1° pixels, ±130° steering on two orthogonal axes per SU; 0.75 cps/Rayleigh sensitivity. Together with the redundant electronic unit and dual filaments per H cell, the 13.25 kg instrument is engineered for >5 yr of L1 operation. / 5×5° FOV·1° 화소·±130° 조향과 두 SU로 1일 미만에 전 천구를 매핑하며, 이중화 설계로 5년 이상의 L1 운용에 대비.

6. **The hydrogen absorption cell (DASS) gives spectral information without a spectrometer / 수소 흡수 셀(DASS)은 분광기 없이 분광 정보를 준다** — Heating a tungsten filament dissociates H₂ → 2H, creating a ~20 mÅ absorption notch at line center. Combined with SOHO's annually varying Doppler shift relative to the LISM flow, the reduction factor R(θ,φ) over many directions yields the IP Lyα line profile by deconvolution — temperature, bulk velocity, possible heliopause perturbations. / 텅스텐 필라멘트로 H₂를 분해해 만든 좁은 흡수띠와 SOHO의 연중 변하는 도플러 시프트를 조합하면, R(θ,φ) 매핑을 deconvolution하여 분광기 없이 라인 프로파일·온도·속도를 얻는다.

7. **SWAN bridges Prognoz inversion (1976) and Ulysses in-situ (1994) / SWAN은 Prognoz 역산과 Ulysses 현장 측정을 잇는다** — Figs. 7 (Prognoz reanalysis) and 8 (Ulysses) show qualitatively the same shape: narrow equatorial high-flux band + broad ±30°–70° plateau ~35–50 % below ecliptic. SWAN's role is to monitor this profile *continuously* through the solar cycle. / 두 그림에서 좁은 적도 고플럭스대와 ±30–70°의 넓은 평탄대(35–50 % 결손)가 모두 보이며, SWAN의 역할은 이 분포를 활동주기 전체에 걸쳐 추적하는 것이다.

8. **Multiple-scattering radiative transfer must be included / 다중 산란 복사 전달을 반드시 포함해야 한다** — Despite τ < 0.1/AU, the strong upwind/downwind density asymmetry produces 1.03–1.30× corrections (Quémerais & Bertaux 1993). The downwind correction is largest where SW-anisotropy effects are also largest, so naive optically-thin analysis biases the inferred latitudinal profile. / τ < 0.1/AU에도 불구하고 상풍/하풍 비대칭으로 1.03–1.30배의 보정이 필요하며, 보정이 큰 하풍 영역과 SW 이방성 영역이 겹치므로 광학적 얇음 가정은 위도 프로파일을 왜곡한다.

## 4. Mathematical Summary / 수학적 요약

### 4.1 Forward Model / 순방향 모델

**Steady-state H density along the upwind axis (Eq. 2):**

$$n(r) = n_\infty\, e^{-r_c/r}$$

- $n_\infty$: H density at infinity (before any ionization), typically 0.1 cm⁻³.
- 1 AU에서 e⁻¹ 감소 거리 / Distance at which H is depleted by 1/e: r_c.

**Penetration depth (Eq. 3):**

$$r_c = \beta_o \frac{r_e^2}{v}$$

- $\beta_o$: 1-AU isotropic ionization rate (~10⁻⁶ to 10⁻⁷ s⁻¹).
- $v$: incoming H velocity (≈20 km/s).
- $r_e$ = 1 AU.

**Travel-time / lifetime ratio (Eq. 4):**

$$\frac{r_c}{r_e} = \frac{T_e}{T_D}$$

- $T_e = r_e/v$: time to traverse 1 AU at v (≈87 days for 20 km/s).
- $T_D = 1/\beta_o$: ionization lifetime.

**Maximum emissivity location (Eq. 5):**

$$r_{max} = \frac{r_c}{2}$$

도출: ε(r) ∝ n(r)/r² = n_∞ e⁻ʳᶜ/ʳ / r². dε/dr = 0 → r_c/r² × n_∞ e⁻ʳᶜ/ʳ /r² − 2 n(r)/r³ = 0 → r_c = 2r → r_max = r_c/2. Q.E.D. (Optically thin approximation.)

**Reaction time at MER (Eq. 6):**

$$T(r_{max}) = T_D \left(\frac{r_{max}}{r_e}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{4}\frac{T_e^2}{T_D}$$

핵심: T(r_max) ∝ 1/T_D. 더 긴 H 수명은 더 *느린* 반응 → MER이 멀고 반응이 느림.
Key: T(r_max) ∝ 1/T_D — longer H lifetime → slower reaction (MER farther out).

### 4.2 Anisotropic Ionization Models / 이방성 이온화 모델

**Harmonic model (Eq. 1):**

$$\beta(\lambda, r) = \beta_e \left(1 - A \sin^2\lambda\right)\frac{r_e^2}{r^2}$$

- $\lambda$: heliographic latitude.
- $A$: anisotropy factor (Prognoz: A ≈ 0.40).
- Total ionization rate at 1 AU on the equator: $\beta_e$.
- $\beta(0,r_e) = \beta_e$, $\beta(\pm 90°, r_e) = \beta_e(1−A)$.
- Latitudinal asymmetry: 40 % at the poles vs. equator.

**Mass flux retrieval pipeline:**

$$\beta_{cx}(\lambda) = n_p(\lambda) \, v_{rel}(\lambda) \, \sigma_{cx}(v_{rel})$$

with the Fite et al. (1962) cross-section

$$\sigma_{cx}(v) \approx \left[a - b \log_{10} v\right]^2 \times 10^{-16}\ \text{cm}^2$$

(decreasing with v). Subtracting the photoionization rate $\beta_{ph}(\lambda) \approx \text{const}$ from the total $\beta(\lambda)$, the SW mass flux $\phi(\lambda) = n_p(\lambda) v_p(\lambda) r_e^2$ (per AU²) is

$$\phi(\lambda) = \frac{\beta_{cx}(\lambda)}{\sigma_{cx}(v_{rel}(\lambda))} \cdot \frac{v_p(\lambda)}{v_{rel}(\lambda)}$$

with $v_{rel} \approx v_p$ (since H atoms are slow). The latitudinal velocity profile $v_p(\lambda)$ comes from external IPS (Kojima & Kakinuma 1987).

### 4.3 Photometry Equation / 광도 방정식

$$N_p(1°) = I \cdot S \cdot \Omega \cdot T_m^2 \cdot T_c \cdot \rho_c \cdot Q$$

| Symbol | Value | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| $I$ | 1 R | 1 Rayleigh = 10⁶/4π photon cm⁻² s⁻¹ ster⁻¹ |
| $S$ | 5.72 cm² | Entrance pupil |
| $\Omega$ | 3×10⁻⁴ sr | 1° pixel solid angle |
| $T_m^2$ | 0.60 | 2-mirror reflectivity |
| $T_c$ | 0.20 | Cell transmission at Lyα |
| $\rho_c$ | 0.5 | Counting efficiency |
| $Q$ | 0.09 | Photocathode quantum efficiency |

**Result: $N_p(1°, 1\text{R}) = 0.75$ counts/s.**

For 500 R IP signal, 45 s integration → 17 000 counts → 0.8 % statistics.

**For a star (Eq. 7):**

$$N^* = \int F_\lambda \, S \, T_\lambda \, Q \, T_m^2 \, T_c \, d\lambda$$

with $F_\lambda$ in photon cm⁻² s⁻¹ nm⁻¹.

### 4.4 Hydrogen Cell DASS / 수소 셀 DASS

**Reduction factor:**

$$R(\theta, \varphi) = \frac{I_{ON}(\theta,\varphi)}{I_{OFF}(\theta,\varphi)} = \frac{\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \phi(\nu) \cdot \tau_{cell}(\nu) d\nu}{\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \phi(\nu) d\nu}$$

where $\phi(\nu)$ is the IP Lyα emission profile (Doppler-shifted by SOHO motion) and $\tau_{cell}(\nu)$ is the cell transmission profile (Gaussian absorption notch FWHM ≈ 39 mÅ).

**Doppler condition for absorption:**

$$|V_D| < V_{cell}, \quad V_D = V_r \cos\alpha$$

with $V_r$ the spacecraft–LISM relative velocity (10–50 km/s) and $\alpha$ the angle between line-of-sight and $\vec{V}_r$. For α=90° (perpendicular), $V_D = 0$ → maximum absorption (R minimum). The locus of perpendicular directions is the *Zero Doppler Shift Circle* (ZDSC).

**Worked numerical example (MER reaction time):**

Given: T_D = 1.5×10⁶ s ≈ 17.4 days; T_e = 87 days at v=20 km/s.

- r_c/r_e = T_e/T_D = 87/17.4 = 5.0 AU. ✓ (Table II row 2)
- r_max = r_c/2 = 2.5 AU. ✓
- T(r_max) = (1/4)(87)²/17.4 = (1/4)·435 = 108 days. ✓ (Table II: 107)

Now suppose ionization doubles (T_D → 8.7 days): r_c → 10 AU, r_max → 5 AU, T(r_max) → 217 days... Wait: T(r_max) = T_e²/(4T_D), so T_D smaller → T(r_max) *larger*. Let me recompute: T_D = 8.7 days → T(r_max) = 87²/(4×8.7) = 7569/34.8 = 217 days. Hmm — but Table II shows the *opposite* trend (T(r_max) decreasing with T_D). 

Re-read: Table II: T_D = 10⁶ s, T(r_max) = 160 days; T_D = 2.5×10⁶ s, T(r_max) = 64 days. So smaller T_D → larger T(r_max), confirming the formula. The author's text says "fast variations are better reflected when the SW flux has been small for a long time, because H atoms penetrate nearer the Sun and react faster." This means: small SW flux ⇒ small β ⇒ large T_D ⇒ small T(r_max) ⇒ fast reaction. ✓

Verifying: T_D = 2.5×10⁶ s = 29 days → T(r_max) = 87²/(4×29) = 7569/116 = 65 days. ✓ Matches Table II.

### 4.5 Radiative Transfer Correction / 복사 전달 보정

For an axisymmetric H distribution, the correction factor is

$$q(\hat{n}) = \frac{I_{multi-scatter}(\hat{n})}{I_{single-scatter}(\hat{n})}$$

ranging from q = 1.03 (upwind) to 1.30 (downwind). The angular dependence of q is monotonic with the angle from the upwind direction.
