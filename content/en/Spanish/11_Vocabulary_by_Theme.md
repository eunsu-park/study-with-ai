## 1. Diminutives and Augmentatives

In Spanish, suffixes are added to nouns, adjectives, and adverbs to express nuances such as size, affection, or contempt.

#### Diminutive Formation Rules

**1. Words ending in a vowel:**
- Drop vowel + -ito/-ita
- casa → casita
- perro → perrito

**2. Words ending in a consonant:**
- As is + -ito/-ita (or -cito/-cita)
- amor → amorcito
- pan → panecito (e inserted)

**3. Words ending in -co/-ca:**
- c → qu + -ito/-ita
- poco → poquito
- chico → chiquito

**4. Words ending in -go/-ga:**
- g → gu + -ito/-ita
- amigo → amiguito
- largo → larguito

**5. One-syllable words:**
- -ecito/-ecita
- flor → florecita
- pan → panecito
- pez → pececito

### C. Nuance Summary of Diminutives/Augmentatives

| Use | Example | Meaning |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **Affection** | Mi hijito / My dear son | Loving |
| **Size** | Una casita / A small house | Physical size |
| **Softening request** | ¿Me das un favorcito? / Could you do me a small favor? | Reducing burden |
| **Irony** | ¡Qué bonito! (sarcastic tone) / How nice! (sarcastically) | Irony |
| **Contempt** | Es un libraco / It's a terrible book | Negative evaluation |
| **Emphasis** | Está cerquita / It's really close | Degree emphasis |
| **Friendliness** | ¿Tomamos un cafecito? / Shall we have coffee? | Comfort |

## Study Tips

1. **Diminutive Practice**: Use diminutives to express friendliness in everyday conversation
2. **Augmentative Caution**: Augmentatives often have negative nuances, context is crucial
3. **Regional Differences**: Diminutive preferences vary by region (Mexico: -ito, Caribbean: -ico)
4. **Idiom Context**: Always learn idioms with context
5. **Modern Vocabulary**: Acquire latest vocabulary through social media and news
