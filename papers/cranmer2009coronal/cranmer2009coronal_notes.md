# 17. Coronal Holes / 코로나 홀

---

## 1. Core Contribution / 핵심 기여

### English
Cranmer's 2009 *Living Reviews* article is the modern reference work on **coronal holes** — the dim, low-density regions of open magnetic flux that feed the **fast solar wind** ($v \gtrsim 600$ km/s). Written at the end of three decades of rapid progress — Skylab (1973), Yohkoh (1991), SOHO/UVCS (1995), Hinode (2006), and the extended 2007–2009 solar minimum — the review synthesizes three parallel strands of evidence: (i) **on-disk diagnostics** in UV/X-ray showing that coronal holes have lower density, cooler electrons, unipolar photospheric magnetic flux, and near-photospheric FIP (First Ionization Potential) signatures; (ii) **off-limb remote sensing** via white-light coronagraphy and UV spectroscopy (UVCS, SUMER) showing $T_{\perp}(\mathrm{O}^{5+}) \gtrsim 2 \times 10^8$ K — one hundred times hotter than the solar core and with extreme anisotropy $T_{\perp} \gg T_{\parallel}$; and (iii) **physics-based models** of how the fast wind gets heated and accelerated. The paper crystallizes the central open question as a choice between two paradigms: **wave/turbulence-driven (WTD)** models in which continuously driven Alfvén waves partially reflect and cascade to small scales, and **reconnection/loop-opening (RLO)** models in which intermittent reconnection between closed loops and the open-field funnel injects Poynting flux and mass. Cranmer shows quantitatively that both can reproduce the fast/slow wind dichotomy but makes the case that the WTD channel — in particular the form $Q \approx \rho Z^3 / L_\perp \propto B$ — explains the broadest set of observables (outflow speed vs. flux-tube expansion, specific entropy $T/n^{\gamma-1}$ vs. wind speed, and empirical charge-state/FIP trends). The kinetic microphysics remains unsettled: ion-cyclotron resonance neatly explains the UVCS heavy-ion signatures but requires either *base generation* (reconnection-produced high-frequency waves) or *gradual generation* (nonlinear cascade from low-frequency to cyclotron frequencies) — with ~15 candidate mechanisms surveyed in the paper's Table 1.

### 한국어
Cranmer의 2009년 *Living Reviews* 논문은 **코로나 홀**에 관한 현대적 참고 문헌의 결정판입니다. 코로나 홀은 광학적으로 어둡고 저밀도인 열린 자기장 영역으로, **고속 태양풍**($v \gtrsim 600$ km/s)을 공급합니다. Skylab(1973), Yohkoh(1991), SOHO/UVCS(1995), Hinode(2006), 그리고 2007–2009년 연장된 태양 극소기를 거친 30년간의 급속한 진보의 끝에 쓰인 이 리뷰는 세 갈래의 증거를 종합합니다: (i) **디스크 상(on-disk) 진단** — UV/X-ray에서 코로나 홀이 저밀도, 낮은 전자 온도, **단극 자속(unipolar flux)**, 그리고 광구에 가까운 FIP 서명을 갖는다는 사실. (ii) **디스크 밖(off-limb) 원격 관측** — white-light coronagraphy와 UV 분광(UVCS, SUMER)을 통해 $T_{\perp}(\mathrm{O}^{5+}) \gtrsim 2 \times 10^8$ K로 관측되었는데, 이는 **태양 중심부보다 100배 뜨거운 온도**이며 극심한 비등방성 $T_{\perp} \gg T_{\parallel}$를 보입니다. (iii) **물리 기반 모델** — 고속풍의 가열·가속 메커니즘. 핵심 미해결 쟁점은 두 패러다임의 대립으로 정식화됩니다: **파동/난류 구동(WTD)** 모델(연속적으로 구동된 Alfvén파가 부분 반사되어 소규모로 캐스케이드)과 **재연결/루프 열림(RLO)** 모델(닫힌 루프와 열린 퍼널 사이의 간헐적 재연결이 Poynting flux와 질량을 주입). Cranmer는 두 모델 모두 고속/저속 풍 이분성을 재현할 수 있음을 정량적으로 보이지만, 특히 $Q \approx \rho Z^3 / L_\perp \propto B$ 형태로 표현되는 **WTD 경로**가 가장 광범위한 관측(유출 속도 vs. 자속관 발산률; 비엔트로피 $T/n^{\gamma-1}$ vs. 풍속; 경험적 전하상태·FIP 경향)을 설명한다고 주장합니다. 그러나 **kinetic microphysics**는 여전히 해결되지 않았습니다: 이온-사이클로트론 공명은 UVCS의 중이온 서명을 깔끔하게 설명하지만, *기저 생성*(재연결에서 발생하는 고주파 파) 또는 *점진 생성*(저주파에서 사이클로트론 주파수까지의 비선형 캐스케이드)을 필요로 합니다 — 논문의 Table 1에는 15가지 후보 메커니즘이 정리되어 있습니다.

## 2. Reading Notes / 읽기 노트

### Part I: Introduction and Three Meanings of "Coronal Hole" (§1) / 도입 — "코로나 홀"의 세 가지 의미 (§1)

**English.** Cranmer opens by noting that the same term "coronal hole" actually denotes **three distinct phenomena** that partially overlap but are not identical: (1) **darkest patches on the disk** in UV/X-ray; (2) **lowest-intensity regions above the limb**, in eclipses or coronagraphs; and (3) **theoretical footpoints of time-steady solar wind flows** (i.e., all open flux). This paper uses definitions (1) and (2) — the observational ones. Three reasons make CHs uniquely interesting: **(i) ambient time-steady state** — easier to model than active regions; **(ii) lowest density ⇒ most collisionless** — ideal testbed for kinetic plasma processes; **(iii) high-speed streams cause ~fraction of geomagnetic storms** via Corotating Interaction Regions (CIRs) at 1 AU.

**한국어.** Cranmer는 "코로나 홀"이라는 용어가 실제로는 부분적으로 겹치되 동일하지 않은 **세 가지 현상**을 지칭한다는 점을 먼저 지적합니다: (1) UV/X-ray에서 **디스크의 가장 어두운 영역**, (2) 일식·coronagraph에서 **림 위 최저 밝기 영역**, (3) **시간-정상 태양풍의 이론적 발자취**(즉 모든 열린 자속). 이 논문은 관측적 정의인 (1)(2)를 주로 사용합니다. CH가 독특하게 흥미로운 세 가지 이유: **(i) 주변(ambient) 시간-정상 상태** — 활동영역보다 모델링이 쉬움; **(ii) 최저 밀도 → 가장 무충돌(collisionless)** — kinetic 과정 연구의 이상적 시험장; **(iii) 고속풍 스트림이 CIRs를 통해 1 AU에서 지자기 폭풍의 상당 부분을 유발**.

### Part II: Historical Overview (§2) / 역사적 개관 (§2, pp. 7–9)

**English.** Naked-eye eclipse drawings from the 20th century already show polar rays and "grooves." Waldmeier (1956, 1957) made the first quantitative observations using the 5303 Å green emission line at Zürich, calling the features *Löcher* (holes) and *Rinne* (grooves). The term "coronal hole" was coined for on-disk dark patches by Newkirk (1967) and Tousey (1968). **Three milestones in 1973** cemented the modern view:
- Munro & Withbroe — reduced density and electron temperature
- Krieger, Timothy & Roelof — first connection of CHs to high-speed streams at 1 AU
- Skylab X-ray images — all prior claims confirmed with vastly improved quality

A notable figure (Fig. 1) contrasts a modern eclipse (1998) with an 1872 sketch by Piddington — showing that the "helmet streamer / polar plume" morphology was already recognized before 1900. The 1978–1982 rocket-borne UV coronagraph spectrometer (Kohl et al.) established proton heating and supersonic outflow in coronal holes, foreshadowing UVCS.

**한국어.** 20세기 초반의 육안 일식 스케치에도 이미 극 rays와 "홈(grooves)"이 보입니다. Waldmeier(1956, 1957)는 Zürich에서 5303 Å 녹색 방출선을 사용해 최초의 정량적 관측을 했으며, 이 특징을 *Löcher*(holes), *Rinne*(grooves)라 불렀습니다. "coronal hole"이라는 용어는 Newkirk(1967)과 Tousey(1968)가 디스크 상 어두운 영역에 처음 사용했습니다. **1973년의 세 가지 이정표**가 현대적 관점을 확립했습니다:
- Munro & Withbroe — 낮은 밀도·전자온도 확인
- Krieger, Timothy & Roelof — CH와 1 AU 고속풍 스트림의 최초 연결
- Skylab X-ray 사진 — 모든 이전 주장의 대폭 향상된 관측 확증

Fig. 1은 1998년 현대 일식 사진과 1872년 Piddington의 스케치를 병치하여, "helmet streamer / 극 plume" 형태학이 1900년 이전에 이미 인식되었음을 보여줍니다. 1978–1982년 로켓 UV coronagraph(Kohl et al.)는 코로나 홀에서 proton 가열과 초음속 유출을 확립하여 UVCS의 선구가 되었습니다.

### Part III: On-Disk Properties (§3) / 디스크 상 특성 (§3, pp. 10–13)

**Key diagnostics / 핵심 진단.**

| Diagnostic / 진단 | Coronal hole signature / 코로나 홀 서명 |
|---|---|
| UV/X-ray intensity | Lowest on disk / 디스크 최저 |
| Chromospheric He I 10830 Å | **Weakest absorption** (reduced He⁰ source function) / 최소 흡수 |
| Elemental abundances | Near-**photospheric** (no strong FIP enhancement) / 광구에 가까움 |
| Photospheric magnetogram | **Unipolar flux imbalance** / 단극 자속 불균형 |
| Flux emergence rate | Lower in unipolar regions / 단극 영역에서 낮음 |
| Life-time | 7 yrs around solar min; absent for 3–4 yrs around max |

**English.** One of the most striking facts is that CHs are **indistinguishable from their surroundings in the photosphere and low chromosphere** — the dark contrast only appears when $T \gtrsim 10^5$ K. The unipolarity is structurally important: the photospheric bright points (1–2 kG strong-field flux tubes) concentrate in supergranular network lanes (intergranular scale ~0.002 $R_\odot$ → network scale ~0.04 $R_\odot$), and between the network lanes the field spreads laterally into "funnels" that ultimately merge into the nearly uniform ~100 G vertical open flux (Fig. 4). The lower emergence rate of ephemeral bipoles in unipolar regions (Abramenko 2006a; Hagenaar 2008) is one reason CHs are **cool and dark** — less total free magnetic energy available for heating. Transient coronal holes (= "coronal dimmings") accompany CME liftoff and show temporary CH-like properties.

**한국어.** 가장 인상적인 사실 중 하나는, CH가 **광구와 하부 채층에서는 주변과 구분되지 않는다**는 것입니다 — 어두운 대비는 $T \gtrsim 10^5$ K일 때만 나타납니다. 단극성(unipolarity)은 구조적으로 중요합니다: 광구 bright points (1–2 kG 강자속관)가 초입자 네트워크 차선에 집중되며(intergranular 스케일 ~0.002 $R_\odot$ → 네트워크 스케일 ~0.04 $R_\odot$), 네트워크 차선 사이에서 자속이 측면으로 퍼지며 "funnel"을 형성하여 최종적으로 거의 균일한 ~100 G 수직 열린 자속으로 합쳐집니다 (Fig. 4). 단극 영역에서 **ephemeral bipole 출현율이 낮다**는 사실(Abramenko 2006a; Hagenaar 2008)은 CH가 **차갑고 어두운** 한 이유입니다 — 가열에 사용 가능한 총 자유 자기 에너지가 적음. 일시적 코로나 홀(= "coronal dimming")은 CME 분출과 동반되며 일시적으로 CH 같은 특성을 보입니다.

### Part IV: Off-Limb Properties (§4) / 디스크 밖 특성 (§4, pp. 14–22)

**§4.1 Magnetic connectivity / 자기적 연결성.** The **PFSS** (Potential Field Source Surface, typically $R_{ss} = 2.5$–$3.5 R_\odot$) method extrapolates photospheric magnetograms under the assumption of current-free corona below the source surface (beyond which the field is radial). PFSS broadly maps CHs → fast wind, streamer belts → slow wind, though it struggles with stream-stream interactions. **Large coronal holes superradially expand**: a flux tube's cross-section grows faster than $r^2$, which is the key variable in Wang & Sheeley's (1990) expansion-factor correlation.

**§4.2 Visible-light coronagraphy / 가시광 coronagraphy.** White-light polarization brightness $pB$ measures the line-of-sight integral of electron density weighted by a known scattering function (Thomson scattering). Coronal-hole electron densities at $r \approx 1.5$–$5 R_\odot$ are **a factor of ~10 lower than streamers** (Fig. 5). Mass conservation $\rho u A = \mathrm{const}$ lets one combine $n_e(r)$ with a PFSS expansion factor to derive outflow speed $u(r)$ (Kohl et al. 2006). The fast wind reaches **roughly half of its asymptotic terminal speed by only ~$2$–$4 R_\odot$** — dramatic, rapid acceleration inside the coronagraph's lower field. LASCO also sees nearly continuous low-contrast "blobs" ejected from streamer cusps (Sheeley et al. 1997). Polar plumes contrast with interplumes: plumes are denser but have *narrower line widths*, *lower kinetic temperatures*, and *lower outflow speeds*. Cranmer (1999b) statistical analysis: plume/interplume density ratio ≈ 2, plume filling factor ≈ 25%, interplume fills ~75% — so the **fast wind is dominated by interplume plasma**.

**§4.3 UV spectroscopy / UV 분광.** Here lies the central observational "punch line." Two instruments:
- **SUMER** (on-disk, $r \lesssim 1.3 R_\odot$): resolved electron temperatures $T_e \sim 0.8$ MK in CHs, lower than frozen-in charge-state temperatures from in situ measurements — a lingering inconsistency resolved only by non-Maxwellian $e^-$ distributions or strong differential flows.
- **UVCS** (off-limb coronagraph + UV spectrometer, $r \approx 1.5$–$10 R_\odot$): this is where physics changed. O VI 1032/1037 Å doublet, Mg X 625 Å, H I Lyα 1216 Å are "brightest" spectral lines.

**The UVCS revolution / UVCS 혁명.** Figure 6 of the paper (critical to understand) shows $T$ vs. $(r/R_\odot - 1)$ with three groups:
- **Electrons** (blue bars, SUMER): $T_e \lesssim 1$ MK, falling slightly with height
- **Protons** (red diamonds, UVCS): $T_p \sim 2$–$3.5$ MK, significantly higher than $T_e$
- **O⁺⁵ ions** (green circles): $T_\perp \gtrsim 100$–$200$ MK above $r \approx 2 R_\odot$ — **hotter than the solar core** ($\sim 15$ MK).

**Ion anisotropy / 이온 비등방성.** $T_{\perp i}/T_{\parallel i} \gtrsim 10$ for O⁺⁵ above $r \approx 2.1 R_\odot$ (Cranmer 2008 reanalysis). Furthermore, heavy-ion outflow speeds *exceed proton speeds* by up to a factor of two — consistent with ion-cyclotron heating inflating the perpendicular temperature, which (together with magnetic mirror expansion) converts perpendicular thermal energy into outflow kinetic energy.

**Figure 8 of paper / 그림 8 (pp. 20).** Shows wind velocity vs. $r/R_\odot$: fast wind (solid black curve) accelerates from ~100 km/s at $r = 2 R_\odot$ to ~750 km/s asymptotically; slow wind (dashed) grows from ~50 km/s more gradually. The fast-wind curve matches the UVCS Doppler-dimming inferred speeds within error bars.

**한국어.** 이 절의 핵심 — UVCS의 "결정타"를 요약하면: O⁺⁵ 이온은 양성자보다 훨씬 **무겁기** 때문에(질량 16배), 전통적인 Maxwell–Boltzmann 가열로는 $T(\mathrm{O}^{5+})$가 질량에 비례해야 합니다 — 즉 $T(\mathrm{O}^{5+}) \sim 16 T_p$. 그러나 관측은 $T_\perp(\mathrm{O}^{5+}) \gtrsim 100 T_p$. 이 "질량-과잉 가열(mass-disproportionate heating)"은 이온의 charge-to-mass 비에 의존하는 메커니즘을 요구 — 이온-사이클로트론 공명이 정확히 그러합니다.

### Part IV-bis: Plumes, Jets, and Coronal Dimmings / 플룸·제트·코로나 dimming

**Polar plumes (§4.2 close) / 극 플룸.** Plumes are thin, ray-like, ~2× denser than interplume surroundings at $r \approx 2 R_\odot$, and rooted in unipolar magnetic flux concentrations. Key spectroscopic properties:
- *Narrower* UVCS line widths → lower kinetic temperatures than interplume / UVCS 선폭이 좁음 → interplume보다 낮은 kinetic 온도
- Lower Doppler-dimming outflow speeds for O⁺⁵ and H⁰ / O⁺⁵, H⁰ 유출 속도가 낮음
- Disappear by $r \approx 60 R_\odot$ (lateral expansion + MHD instabilities mix them into interplume)

Wang (1994, 1998) successfully modeled plumes as flux tubes with **concentrated base heating** (X-ray bright points, nanoflare-like reconnection). The extra base heat raises base density; but by gas-pressure scale-height argument, the heating rate per unit mass (and thus acceleration) in the extended corona is *reduced*, giving slower/denser plume wind. Practically this means **polar plumes are NOT the main fast-wind conduit** — they are *passengers* on the interplume substrate. / Wang(1994, 1998)은 플룸을 **집중된 기저 가열**(X-ray bright points, 나노플레어식 재연결)을 가진 자속관으로 성공적으로 모델링. 추가 기저 가열은 기저 밀도를 높이지만, 가스 압력 스케일 높이 논증에 의해 확장 코로나에서의 입자당 가열률(따라서 가속)은 *감소*하여 플룸 풍은 느리고 밀집. 즉 **극 플룸은 주된 고속풍 통로가 아님** — interplume 기질 위의 *승객*.

**Polar jets / 극 제트.** Hinode+LASCO discovered transient jets with two populations: **cool jets** (higher density, lower T, faster outflow) and **hot jets** (lower density, hotter). Dobrzycka et al. (2000, 2006) and Raouafi et al. (2008) argue the same basal reconnection engine drives both plumes (long-duration bursts) and jets (short bursts) — plumes are essentially "time-averaged jets" on the same flux tubes. This observational link directly supports RLO contributing to fast-wind mass flux, even if not the dominant heating mechanism. / Hinode+LASCO가 두 종류의 일시적 제트를 발견: **cool jets**(고밀도, 저온, 빠른 유출)와 **hot jets**(저밀도, 고온). 동일한 기저 재연결 엔진이 플룸(장시간 버스트)과 제트(단시간 버스트)를 모두 구동 — 플룸은 본질적으로 동일 자속관 위의 "시간 평균된 제트". RLO가 고속풍 질량 플럭스에 기여함을 관측적으로 지지 (지배적 가열 메커니즘이 아닐 수 있더라도).

**Transient coronal holes (coronal dimmings) / 일시적 코로나 홀.** Rapid UV/X-ray intensity reductions accompanying CME liftoff. Spectroscopically similar to quasi-steady CHs — Doppler blueshifts and large nonthermal line widths. Aschwanden et al. (2009) began using dimming mass as a proxy for total CME mass, closing the link between CH phenomenology and space-weather forecasting. / CME 분출과 동반되는 급속한 UV/X-ray 감광. 준정상 CH와 분광학적으로 유사 — Doppler 청색편이, 큰 nonthermal 선폭. Aschwanden et al.(2009)는 dimming 질량을 총 CME 질량 대리 변수로 사용 — CH 현상학과 우주기상 예보 간 연결.

**Connection to CIRs and space weather / CIR·우주기상 연결.** High-speed streams from CHs overtake ambient slow wind ahead, forming **Corotating Interaction Regions** (CIRs). CIRs drive **recurrent geomagnetic storms** (every ~27 days matching solar rotation) and long-lasting energetic electron fluxes that are hazardous to geostationary satellites. Predicting CH boundaries on the solar disk is therefore a direct space-weather forecasting task — and Bell & Noci (1973), Neupert & Pizzo (1974), Tsanskanen et al. (2005), and Zhang et al. (2007) developed the observational tools that connect solar imaging to terrestrial magnetic effects. / CH로부터의 고속풍 스트림은 앞의 저속풍을 따라잡으며 **CIR**를 형성. CIR는 **재귀적 지자기 폭풍**(태양 자전과 일치하는 ~27일 주기)과 정지궤도 위성에 위해한 장기 고에너지 전자 플럭스를 구동. 디스크 상 CH 경계 예측은 직접적 우주기상 예보 과제.

### Part V: Coronal Heating and Wind Acceleration (§5) / 가열과 풍 가속 (§5, pp. 23–34)

#### §5.1 Sources of energy / 에너지 원천

The ultimate energy source is the solar **convection zone**, which jostles magnetic flux tubes and pumps MHD waves and magnetic-free-energy up through the photosphere. A small fraction of this mechanical energy reaches the corona. Two broad families:
1. **Wave/Turbulence-Driven (WTD)** — continuous wave fluxes, partial reflection, anisotropic turbulent cascade, dissipation spread over many $R_\odot$.
2. **Reconnection/Loop-Opening (RLO)** — intermittent reconnection bursts at/between closed-open field boundaries inject mass and Poynting flux.

**Both reproduce the fast/slow dichotomy**, so neither can be eliminated from observations alone — but they predict different sub-patterns.

#### §5.2 Wave/Turbulence-Driven model — ZEPHYR / WTD — ZEPHYR 모델

Cranmer et al. (2007)'s ZEPHYR code solves one-fluid MHD conservation equations + Alfvénic and acoustic wave transport, self-consistently from photosphere to $\sim 1$ AU. The empirical Alfvén-wave power spectrum at the photospheric base is taken from G-band bright-point horizontal motions (periods ~5–10 minutes). Non-WKB transport gives the Elsässer amplitudes $Z_\pm$ (upward/downward wave energy), and **the heating rate** is the paper's central equation:

$$
Q = \rho \left( \frac{1}{1 + [t_{\mathrm{eddy}}/t_{\mathrm{ref}}]^n} \right) \frac{Z_{-}^2 Z_{+} + Z_{+}^2 Z_{-}}{4 L_\perp}
\quad\text{(Eq. 1 of paper)}
$$

- $\rho$: mass density
- $L_\perp$: perpendicular correlation length of turbulence, scaling as flux-tube cross-section $L_\perp \propto B^{-1/2}$
- The parenthesis term is a "cascade efficiency" that quenches heating when eddy time $t_{\mathrm{eddy}}$ is much longer than wave-reflection time $t_{\mathrm{ref}}$.

For open-field regions, Cranmer et al. used $n = 1$. When the quenching factor is ignored, Eq. (1) simplifies to:

$$
Q \approx \frac{\rho Z^3}{L_\perp} \approx Q_\star f_\star \propto \frac{B}{B_\star}
\quad\text{(Eq. 2 of paper)}
$$

**This is striking:** $Q \propto B$ — **coronal heating scales linearly with magnetic flux density at the base**. This matches a large body of observations (Pevtsov 2003, Schwadron 2006, Suzuki 2006) in both stars and the Sun. $f_\star$ is the area filling factor of bright points at the photosphere; it varies ~50× across the surface.

**ZEPHYR key results (Fig. 9 of paper):**
- Latitudinal wind-speed bifurcation at $\sim 2$ AU ($v \approx 750$ km/s at poles vs. $\approx 400$ km/s at equator) matches Ulysses 1994–1995 polar pass
- Coronal temperature profile peaks at ~1.5 MK near $1$–$2 R_\odot$ (Fig. 9c)
- Empirical FIP fractionation and O⁷⁺/O⁶⁺ charge state trends reproduced **solely from varying magnetic geometry** — same photospheric boundary, different wind outcomes

**Entropy test (Fig. 10 of paper):** Specific entropy proxy $\ln(T_p / n_p^{\gamma-1})$ vs. wind speed — both ZEPHYR models and ACE/SWEPAM in situ data show positive correlation across 300–800 km/s. Entropy is nearly conserved in the absence of dissipation, so 1-AU entropy is a long-distance tracer of near-Sun heating — and the model tracks the data well.

#### §5.3 Reconnection/Loop-Opening model / 재연결·루프 열림 모델

The "magnetic carpet" (Schrijver 1997; Title & Schrijver 1998; Hagenaar 1999) — continuous emergence/separation/merging of small-scale bipoles — provides a natural energy reservoir. Since only a small fraction of photospheric flux actually opens to the heliosphere (Close et al. 2003), most reconnection events are between closed loops. The RLO proposal: **stochastic reconnection between closed and open flux tubes is the dominant channel**. Correlations between loop length, low-corona $T_e$, and 1-AU wind speed (Feldman 1999; Gloeckler 2003) suggest a net Poynting-flux transfer from loops to open field. Testing RLO is harder than WTD because it requires fully 3D multi-scale models. Polar jets (§4) may be a smoking gun — they show short-lived reconnection-like bursts that could seed both jets and plumes.

#### §5.4 Kinetic microphysics — the cyclotron question / 이온-사이클로트론 수수께끼

**The core problem.** Ion-cyclotron resonance requires wave frequencies $\omega \sim \Omega_i \sim 10^2$–$10^4$ Hz. But photospheric Alfvén waves are believed to have $\omega \lesssim 10^{-2}$ Hz (periods of minutes–hours). So either:
- **"Base generation":** high-frequency waves produced by small-scale reconnection in the network "furnace" (Axford & McKenzie 1992) propagate upward and resonate with ions when $B$ decreases enough that $\Omega_i$ matches.
- **"Gradual generation":** a nonlinear MHD turbulent cascade transfers power from low to high frequencies over many $R_\odot$.

Cranmer (2001) showed base-generated cyclotron waves would be "absorbed" by ions with $q/m$ ratios smaller than the observed O⁺⁵ ($q/m = 0.31$), so the spectrum reaching O⁺⁵ height should be depleted — casting doubt on pure base generation.

The difficulty with gradual generation: **turbulent cascade in MHD is strongly anisotropic** — power transfers preferentially to high $k_\perp$ (kinetic Alfvén waves, KAWs), leaving $k_\parallel$ (and hence $\omega$) nearly unchanged (Strauss 1976; Goldreich & Sridhar 1995). KAWs preferentially heat *electrons parallel to the field* — the **opposite** of what UVCS observes. So the question becomes: can nonlinear/multi-step processes convert a perpendicular-cascade to ion perpendicular heating?

**Cranmer's Table 1 — 15+ candidate processes** categorized into:
1. **Reconnection events** generate: ion-cyclotron Alfvén waves, electron beams → IC waves, fast collisionless shocks
2. **Extended-corona MHD turbulence** generates: parallel cascade, three-wave coupling, Fermi-like diffusion, shear instabilities, nonlinear wave-particle resonances, Debye-scale electron holes, oblique MHD waves, current sheets, transverse density gradients
3. **Low-frequency Alfvén waves in the corona** undergo: polarization drift → lower-hybrid waves, nonresonant stochastic heating, fractional cyclotron stochastic heating
4. **Heavy-ion velocity filtration** (Scudder 1992) — suprathermal tails in the chromosphere preferentially survive into the corona

**Conclusion of §5.** Observations do not yet pin down a unique mechanism, but several independent constraints (specific entropy, outflow vs. expansion, O⁺⁵ anisotropy, polar-jet phenomenology) increasingly point to **WTD + nonlinear cyclotron coupling** as a working picture.

### Part VI: Summary, Broader Context, and Stellar Analogs (§6) / 요약과 항성 비교 (§6, pp. 35–36)

**English.** The review closes with an unusually broad perspective: CHs illuminate **stellar outflows and star/planet evolution** too. Figure 11 shows the evolution of a solar-mass star across Protostar (bipolar outflows) → CTTS (disk-driven jets) → WTTS (X-ray-bright) → ZAMS (dark polar spots) → present-day Sun (CHs). The physics of open-field regions runs through all these stages, with analogies to *polar wind* from Earth's ionosphere. Open questions: which wave modes (Alfvén/fast/slow) dominate? Linear vs. nonlinear? High $k_\parallel$ or $k_\perp$? What fraction of heliospheric wind comes from plume/jet filamentary structures? Can "scaling laws" connect photospheric properties to downstream wind? **Future missions** (Solar Probe, Solar Orbiter) and next-gen UV spectrocoronagraphs are needed to settle the question.

**한국어.** 리뷰는 예상외로 넓은 시야에서 마무리됩니다: CH는 **항성 유출과 별/행성 진화** 문제도 비춥니다. Fig. 11은 태양 질량 항성의 진화 단계 — 원시성(양극 유출) → CTTS(원반 구동 제트) → WTTS(X-ray 밝음) → ZAMS(어두운 극 반점) → 현재 태양(CH) — 를 보여주며, 열린 자기장 영역의 물리가 모든 단계를 관통함을 시사합니다. 지구 전리층의 *polar wind*와도 유비적 관계가 있습니다. 미해결 쟁점: 어떤 파 모드(Alfvén/fast/slow)가 지배적인가? 선형 vs. 비선형? 고 $k_\parallel$ vs. $k_\perp$? 플룸·제트 등 필라멘트 구조가 태양권 풍의 어느 만큼을 차지하는가? 광구 특성을 하류 풍속에 연결하는 "스케일링 법칙"이 존재하는가? **미래 임무**(Solar Probe, Solar Orbiter)와 차세대 UV 분광 coronagraph 로부터 답을 기대합니다.

## 3. Key Takeaways / 핵심 시사점

1. **Coronal holes are THE source of the fast solar wind.** / **코로나 홀은 고속 태양풍의 근원.**
   **Why this matters / 의의.** The correlation between large polar coronal holes and $v \gtrsim 600$ km/s streams measured in situ has been solid since Krieger et al. (1973). This single observational fact motivates treating CHs as a controlled laboratory for wind-acceleration physics.
   Krieger et al.(1973) 이래 대형 극 CH와 1 AU에서 관측된 $v \gtrsim 600$ km/s 스트림의 상관관계는 확고합니다. 이 관측 사실 하나가 CH를 풍 가속 물리의 제어된 실험실로 다루는 근거입니다.

2. **CHs are "dark" because they carry mass/energy away.** / **CH는 질량·에너지를 우주로 빼내기 때문에 어둡다.**
   **Why this matters / 의의.** Pneuman (1973) argued that CHs need not have lower energy *deposition* — the solar wind just carries much of the deposited energy away. Combined with the Abramenko/Hagenaar finding that ephemeral-bipole flux emergence is **lower in unipolar regions**, CHs are low-density both because less heating is delivered *and* because what is delivered escapes as wind.
   Pneuman(1973)은 CH가 낮은 에너지 *주입*을 필요로 하지 않고, 태양풍이 주입된 에너지의 대부분을 가져간다고 주장. Abramenko/Hagenaar의 발견(단극 영역에서 ephemeral bipole 출현율이 낮음)과 결합하면, CH는 (a) 가열이 적게 전달되고 (b) 전달된 가열도 풍으로 빠져나가기 때문에 저밀도임.

3. **Superradial flux-tube expansion is the critical geometric variable.** / **초발산(superradial) 자속관 발산이 결정적 기하 변수.**
   **Why this matters / 의의.** Wang & Sheeley (1990): wind speed inversely correlates with the flux-tube expansion factor $f_\mathrm{ss}$. Physically, a rapidly expanding (high $f$) tube requires more heating per particle to accelerate, so the wind ends up slower and denser. Large polar CHs have low $f$ → fast, tenuous wind.
   Wang & Sheeley(1990): 풍속은 자속관 발산률 $f_\mathrm{ss}$의 역과 상관. 물리적으로, 급히 발산하는(고 $f$) 관은 입자당 더 많은 가열이 필요해 풍이 느리고 밀집. 대형 극 CH는 낮은 $f$ → 빠르고 희박한 풍.

4. **UVCS measurements of O⁺⁵ $T_\perp > 10^8$ K demand ion-cyclotron-like physics.** / **UVCS의 O⁺⁵ $T_\perp > 10^8$ K는 이온-사이클로트론류 물리를 요구.**
   **Why this matters / 의의.** No Maxwellian thermal mechanism can boost heavy ions to $T_\perp/T_p \gg m_i/m_p$. Ion-cyclotron resonance is the only known mechanism that naturally selects ions by $q/m$ and heats them perpendicularly.
   Maxwell 분포의 열적 메커니즘으로는 중이온을 $T_\perp/T_p \gg m_i/m_p$까지 끌어올릴 수 없음. 이온-사이클로트론 공명만이 $q/m$으로 이온을 선별하고 수직 가열을 자연스럽게 설명.

5. **Fast wind accelerates to half its terminal speed by just $2$–$4 R_\odot$.** / **고속풍은 $2$–$4 R_\odot$ 이내에 종단 속도의 절반에 도달.**
   **Why this matters / 의의.** This is *rapid* — much faster than simple Parker isothermal solutions predict. It implies strong energy deposition at low heights, not distributed over tens of solar radii. Constrains WTD vs. RLO: deposit energy below the Parker critical point to "puff up" the atmosphere (slow + dense wind); deposit above to "pump" kinetic energy (fast + tenuous wind).
   이는 *급격한* 가속 — 단순 Parker 등온 해가 예측하는 것보다 훨씬 빠름. 낮은 고도의 강한 에너지 주입을 의미. Parker 임계점 이하에 주입하면 대기를 "부풀려" 느리고 밀집한 풍을 만듦; 임계점 위에 주입하면 운동 에너지로 "펌프"하여 빠르고 희박한 풍을 만듦.

6. **Coronal heating scales linearly with base magnetic flux density ($Q \propto B$).** / **코로나 가열은 기저 자속 밀도에 선형 비례 ($Q \propto B$).**
   **Why this matters / 의의.** Eq. (2) of the paper — a non-obvious scaling that falls out of ZEPHYR's WTD framework after $L_\perp \propto B^{-1/2}$, $\rho \propto f^2$, $Z \propto \rho^{-1/4}$. Empirically confirmed across solar regions and even cool-star X-ray fluxes (Pevtsov 2003). This is a **predictive scaling** the WTD paradigm offers that RLO does not naturally provide.
   논문 Eq.(2) — ZEPHYR의 WTD 프레임워크에서 $L_\perp \propto B^{-1/2}$, $\rho \propto f^2$, $Z \propto \rho^{-1/4}$ 등을 조합하면 얻어지는 비자명 스케일링. 태양 및 냉항성 X-ray 플럭스에서도 경험적으로 확인(Pevtsov 2003). RLO가 자연스럽게 제공하지 못하는 WTD의 **예측적** 스케일링.

7. **WTD and RLO are not mutually exclusive.** / **WTD와 RLO는 상호 배타적이지 않음.**
   **Why this matters / 의의.** Both models reproduce fast-slow wind dichotomy; both can reproduce FIP/charge-state trends; polar jets show reconnection *and* plumes show steady heating. The combined reality likely has reconnection seeding high-frequency waves that then drive turbulent cascades — i.e., RLO provides the *initial conditions* for WTD physics.
   두 모델 모두 고속-저속 이분성을 재현하고, FIP·전하상태 경향도 재현. 극 제트는 재연결을 보이고 플룸은 정상 가열을 보임. 실제로는 재연결이 고주파 파를 시드로 공급하고 그 파가 난류 캐스케이드를 구동 — 즉 RLO가 WTD 물리의 *초기 조건*을 제공.

8. **CHs are an astrophysical template for stellar outflows.** / **CH는 항성 유출의 천체물리학적 원형.**
   **Why this matters / 의의.** T-Tauri disk winds, WTTS X-ray-bright plasmas, ZAMS rapidly-rotating young stars — all feature open-field regions analogous to solar CHs. Understanding CH physics feeds directly into stellar evolution, angular-momentum loss, and habitability of exoplanets.
   T-Tauri 원반풍, WTTS의 X-ray 플라즈마, ZAMS 고속 자전 영년 주계열성 — 모두 태양 CH에 유비되는 열린 자속 영역을 가짐. CH 물리는 항성 진화, 각운동량 손실, 외계 행성 거주 가능성에 직접 연결.

## 4. Mathematical Summary / 수학적 요약

### 4.1 Parker wind equation (critical point) / Parker 풍 방정식 (임계점)

$$
\frac{1}{u}\frac{du}{dr}\left(u^2 - a^2\right) = \frac{2 a^2}{r} - \frac{G M_\odot}{r^2}
$$

Supersonic critical point at $r_c = G M_\odot / (2 a^2)$ where $u = a$. For coronal hole $T \approx 1.5$ MK, isothermal $a \approx 150$ km/s and $r_c \approx 6 R_\odot$. / 임계점은 $r_c = G M_\odot/(2a^2)$, $u = a$. $T \approx 1.5$ MK → $a \approx 150$ km/s, $r_c \approx 6 R_\odot$.

### 4.2 Flux conservation and superradial expansion / 자속 보존과 초발산

$$
B(r)\,A(r) = \mathrm{const}, \qquad f(r) \equiv \frac{A(r)/r^2}{A(R_\odot)/R_\odot^2}
$$

$f \to 1$ for purely radial expansion; $f > 1$ is *superradial* (large polar holes at solar min: $f_\mathrm{ss} \sim 3$–$10$). Wang & Sheeley: wind speed $v_\infty \propto f_\mathrm{ss}^{-\alpha}$ with $\alpha \sim 1$. / $f \to 1$은 순수 방사 팽창; $f > 1$은 *초발산*(태양 극소기 대형 극 홀: $f_\mathrm{ss} \sim 3$–$10$). Wang & Sheeley: $v_\infty \propto f_\mathrm{ss}^{-\alpha}$, $\alpha \sim 1$.

### 4.3 Mass-flux conservation along flux tube / 자속관 따른 질량 보존

$$
\rho u A = \mathrm{const} \implies \frac{n u}{B} = \mathrm{const}
$$

Combining remote-sensed $n(r)$ with PFSS-extrapolated $B(r)$ gives $u(r)$ without direct velocity measurement. / 원격 관측 $n(r)$과 PFSS 외삽 $B(r)$을 결합하면 속도 직접 관측 없이 $u(r)$ 획득.

### 4.4 Alfvén wave and heating rate (ZEPHYR, Eq. 1) / Alfvén파와 가열률

Alfvén speed:
$$
v_A = \frac{B}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \rho}}
$$

Elsässer variables: $\mathbf{Z}_\pm = \mathbf{v} \mp \mathbf{b}/\sqrt{\mu_0 \rho}$ — $Z_-$ is the upward-propagating wave amplitude, $Z_+$ is the downward (reflected) wave.

Heating rate from Alfvén-wave turbulence:
$$
Q = \rho \left( \frac{1}{1 + [t_\mathrm{eddy}/t_\mathrm{ref}]^n} \right) \frac{Z_-^2 Z_+ + Z_+^2 Z_-}{4 L_\perp}
$$

**Variables / 변수:**
- $\rho$: mass density / 질량 밀도
- $Z_\pm$: Elsässer amplitudes / Elsässer 진폭
- $L_\perp$: perpendicular correlation length, $L_\perp \propto B^{-1/2}$
- $t_\mathrm{eddy} \sim L_\perp / \sqrt{Z_+^2 + Z_-^2}$ / eddy turnover time
- $t_\mathrm{ref}$: wave reflection time / 파 반사 시간
- $n$: cascade-quenching exponent, typically $n = 1$

**Physical meaning / 물리적 의미.** The numerator $Z_-^2 Z_+ + Z_+^2 Z_-$ requires BOTH upward AND downward waves — purely outward waves do not cascade. Reflection is essential. The denominator $L_\perp$ reflects eddy shedding at the perpendicular correlation scale. The quenching factor suppresses heating when eddies cannot develop before waves escape. / 분자는 상향+하향 파 동시 존재를 요구 — 순수 상향 파만으로는 캐스케이드 없음; 반사가 필수. 분모 $L_\perp$는 수직 상관 스케일에서의 eddy shedding. Quenching 인수는 파가 탈출하기 전 eddy 발달 시간 부족 시 가열 억제.

### 4.5 Scaling law: $Q \propto B$ (ZEPHYR, Eq. 2) / 스케일링 법칙

Assuming negligible quenching and using $L_\perp \propto B^{-1/2}$, $\rho \propto f_\star^2 \rho_\star$ (thin flux tube), $Z_\pm \propto \rho^{-1/4}$:

$$
Q \approx \frac{\rho Z^3}{L_\perp} \approx \left(\frac{\rho_\star Z_\star^3}{L_{\perp,\star}}\right) \frac{f_\star^2 f_\star^{-3/2}}{f_\star^{-1/2}} = Q_\star f_\star \propto \frac{B}{B_\star}
$$

**Interpretation / 해석.** Coronal heating rate at the merging height scales *linearly* with mean magnetic flux density $\langle B \rangle = f_\star B_\star$ where $B_\star \approx 1500$ G is the bright-point field strength and $f_\star$ is the filling factor. This explains empirical $L_X \propto \Phi$ scaling for stars (Pevtsov 2003). / 병합 고도에서의 코로나 가열률은 평균 자속 밀도 $\langle B \rangle = f_\star B_\star$에 *선형* 비례 ($B_\star \approx 1500$ G는 bright-point 장 강도, $f_\star$는 충진율). 항성의 $L_X \propto \Phi$ 스케일링(Pevtsov 2003)을 설명.

### 4.6 Ion-cyclotron resonance condition / 이온-사이클로트론 공명 조건

$$
\omega - k_\parallel u_\parallel = \frac{\Omega_i}{\gamma}, \qquad \Omega_i = \frac{q_i B}{m_i c}
$$

For a non-relativistic ion at rest ($\gamma \to 1$, $u_\parallel \to 0$) and a parallel-propagating wave, this reduces to $\omega = \Omega_i$. For O⁺⁵ ($q = 5e$, $m = 16 m_p$, $q/m = 0.31\, e/m_p$) in $B = 1$ G: $\Omega_i \approx 480$ Hz. / 비상대론적 정지 이온과 평행 전파파에 대해 $\omega = \Omega_i$. O⁺⁵, $B = 1$ G에서 $\Omega_i \approx 480$ Hz.

### 4.7 Specific entropy (McComas/Burlaga) / 비엔트로피

$$
s \propto \ln\!\left( \frac{T_p}{n_p^{\gamma-1}} \right), \qquad \gamma \approx 1.5
$$

Conserved in the absence of dissipation — 1-AU entropy is a fingerprint of near-Sun heating. ACE/SWEPAM + ZEPHYR both show monotonic $s(v_\mathrm{sw})$, Fig. 10 of paper. / 소산 없는 경우 보존 — 1 AU 엔트로피는 근태양 가열의 지문. ACE/SWEPAM과 ZEPHYR 모두 단조로운 $s(v_\mathrm{sw})$를 보임 (논문 Fig. 10).

### 4.8 Worked numerical example: Parker critical point in a coronal hole / 수치 예시: CH에서의 Parker 임계점

**Setup / 설정.** Take a representative coronal-hole base with $T = 1.5 \times 10^6$ K and pure hydrogen ($m = m_p$, accounting for electrons $m \to m_p/2$).

**Step 1 — isothermal sound speed.**
$$a = \sqrt{\frac{2 k_B T}{m_p}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot (1.38 \times 10^{-23})(1.5 \times 10^6)}{1.67 \times 10^{-27}}} \approx 1.6 \times 10^5~\text{m/s} = 158~\text{km/s}$$

**Step 2 — critical radius.**
$$r_c = \frac{G M_\odot}{2 a^2} = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(1.99 \times 10^{30})}{2 \cdot (1.6 \times 10^5)^2} \approx 2.6 \times 10^9~\text{m} \approx 3.7~R_\odot$$

**Step 3 — asymptotic speed from Bernoulli.** At $r \to \infty$ with isothermal approximation:
$$u_\infty^2 \approx 4 a^2 \ln\!\left(\frac{r_c}{R_\odot}\right) \approx 4 \cdot (158)^2 \cdot \ln(3.7) \approx 1.3 \times 10^5~(\text{km/s})^2 \implies u_\infty \approx 360~\text{km/s}$$

**Punch line / 결론.** Pure Parker isothermal does **not** reach 700–800 km/s for realistic CH temperatures. The observed fast wind therefore **requires additional energy deposition** beyond simple thermal conduction — exactly the gap that WTD and RLO models fill. A coronal temperature of ~3 MK (closer to UVCS proton temperatures) would give $u_\infty \approx 700$ km/s, but then hydrostatic density scale height makes the base density inconsistent with coronagraph measurements. This tension is why the paper's Fig. 6 (temperatures vs. height) and Fig. 8 (wind speed vs. height) together constrain models so strongly. / 순수 Parker 등온은 현실적 CH 온도로 700–800 km/s에 도달 **못 함**. 관측된 고속풍은 단순 열전도를 넘는 **추가 에너지 주입**을 요구 — WTD와 RLO 모델이 채우는 간극. 3 MK(UVCS proton 온도에 가까움)를 쓰면 $u_\infty \approx 700$ km/s이나, 그 경우 정수압 스케일 높이가 coronagraph 밀도와 모순. 이 긴장이 논문 Fig. 6(온도 vs. 고도)과 Fig. 8(풍속 vs. 고도)이 함께 모델을 강하게 제약하는 이유.

### 4.9 Table 1 — Map of cyclotron heating candidates / 사이클로트론 가열 후보군 지도

Cranmer's Table 1 (p. 30) taxonomizes suggested processes for preferentially heating minor ions. A compressed summary:

| Class / 분류 | Key mechanism / 핵심 메커니즘 | Pro / 장점 | Con / 단점 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Base reconnection** | High-freq IC waves from network furnace | Natural energy source; explains polar jets / 자연스런 에너지; 극 제트 설명 | Wrong ions absorb first; spectrum sculpted / 엉뚱한 이온 먼저 흡수 |
| **Parallel cascade** | $\omega$ transferred from low to high | Fits turbulence phenomenology / 난류 현상학 부합 | Cascade is mostly $k_\perp$ / 캐스케이드 대부분 $k_\perp$ |
| **KAW → IC** | Shear instability, nonlinear resonance | Uses observed $k_\perp$ cascade / 관측된 $k_\perp$ 캐스케이드 활용 | Threshold conditions uncertain / 임계 조건 불확실 |
| **Stochastic heating** | Non-resonant, large-amplitude $V_\mathrm{pol}$ | No gyrofreq requirement / 자이로주파수 불필요 | Max effect is mass-proportional; UVCS exceeds this / 최대 효과가 질량 비례 — UVCS는 이를 초과 |
| **Velocity filtration** | Suprathermal tails survive / 초열 꼬리 생존 | No heating required / 가열 불필요 | Requires chromospheric suprathermal pop. / 채층 초열 분포 필요 |

**Conclusion / 결론.** No single mechanism explains all observables. The paper's final paragraph invites using (i) radio scintillation, (ii) Faraday rotation, and (iii) high-resolution UV line profiles as new constraints — a research agenda that Parker Solar Probe and Solar Orbiter now partly fulfill *in situ*. / 단일 메커니즘으로 모든 관측을 설명할 수 없음. 논문 마지막은 (i) 전파 scintillation, (ii) Faraday 회전, (iii) 고해상도 UV 선 프로파일을 새로운 제약으로 제안 — PSP와 Solar Orbiter가 현재 in situ로 부분 실현 중인 연구 의제.
