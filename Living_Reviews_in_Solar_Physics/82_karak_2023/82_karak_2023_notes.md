# 82. Models for the Long-Term Variations of Solar Activity / 태양 활동의 장기 변동 모델

---

## 1. Core Contribution / 핵심 기여

**English**
Karak (2023) is a comprehensive *Living Reviews in Solar Physics* survey of dynamo models that explain the long-term modulations of solar activity that go beyond the 11-year Schwabe cycle. These modulations include grand minima (Maunder, Spörer, Dalton), grand maxima, the Gnevyshev–Ohl / Even–Odd rule, the Gleissberg cycle (~90–100 yr) and the Suess/de Vries cycle (~205–210 yr). The review condenses more than six decades of theoretical work into three root causes: (1) magnetic feedback on large-scale flows (Lorentz force on differential rotation and meridional circulation, Λ-quenching), (2) stochastic forcing — most importantly fluctuations in the Babcock–Leighton (BL) source arising from Joy's law tilt scatter, anti-Hale and "rogue" bipolar magnetic regions (BMRs) — and (3) time delays intrinsic to spatially segregated poloidal and toroidal source regions. Using the Babcock–Leighton flux-transport dynamo (FTD) as a central workbench, Karak argues that none of these mechanisms alone produces the full observational spectrum: the combination of weakly nonlinear (slightly supercritical) behaviour with stochastic forcing is required. The paper evaluates recent 2D, 2x2D and 3D BL models (Choudhuri & Karak 2012; Lemerle & Charbonneau 2017; Karak & Miesch 2017, 2018; Kumar et al. 2021; Biswas et al. 2022), MHD convection simulations, and low-dimensional iterative-map and time-delay models, and outlines open questions (bimodal activity distribution, origin of Gnevyshev–Ohl, Gleissberg, Suess/de Vries cycles). The conclusion — that the Sun is a weakly supercritical, stochastically driven dynamo — is the guiding framework for modern cycle-prediction efforts.

**한국어**
Karak(2023)은 *Living Reviews in Solar Physics*에 실린 종합 리뷰로, 11년 Schwabe 주기를 넘어서는 태양 활동의 장기 변동 — 그랜드 미니마(Maunder, Spörer, Dalton), 그랜드 맥시마, Gnevyshev–Ohl (Even–Odd) 규칙, 글라이스버그 주기(~90–100 yr), 수에스/드 브리 주기(~205–210 yr) — 을 설명하는 다이나모 모델을 총망라한다. 60여 년 간의 이론 연구를 세 가지 근본 원인으로 축약한다: (1) 대규모 유동(차동 회전·자오선 순환)에 대한 자기 피드백(로렌츠 힘, Λ-quenching), (2) 확률적 강제 — 특히 Joy's law tilt scatter, anti-Hale 및 "rogue" BMR에서 기인하는 Babcock–Leighton(BL) source 요동, (3) poloidal 및 toroidal source 영역이 공간적으로 분리된 다이나모에서 필연적으로 발생하는 시간 지연(time delay). 저자는 BL 플럭스 수송 다이나모(FTD)를 중심 플랫폼으로 삼아, 단일 메커니즘만으로는 관측 전체를 재현할 수 없으며 약한 비선형성(약간 초임계)과 확률적 강제의 조합이 필수임을 강조한다. 최근 2D, 2x2D, 3D BL 모델(Choudhuri & Karak 2012; Lemerle & Charbonneau 2017; Karak & Miesch 2017, 2018; Kumar et al. 2021; Biswas et al. 2022), MHD convection 시뮬레이션, 저차원 iterative map 및 time-delay 모델을 평가하고, 열린 질문(이중봉형 활동 분포, Gnevyshev–Ohl/글라이스버그/수에스 주기의 기원)을 정리한다. "태양은 약하게 초임계이며 확률적으로 구동되는 다이나모"라는 결론은 현대의 주기 예측 연구의 지침이 된다.

## 2. Reading Notes / 읽기 노트

### Part I: Observations of Long-Term Variations / 장기 변동의 관측 (Sect. 1–2, pp. 2–5)

**English**
Sect. 1 motivates the review: the 11-year cycle is not regular — sunspot number and group number time-series (since 1610, Fig. 1) show cycle-to-cycle amplitude variations, and cosmogenic isotopes (¹⁴C, ¹⁰Be) extend this variability back 11,400 years (Fig. 2). Sect. 2 enumerates specific phenomena:

- **Grand minima**: extended low-activity epochs. The Maunder Minimum (1645–1715, ~70 yr) is the canonical example — recent analyses (Beer et al. 1998; Zolotova & Ponyavin 2015; Vaquero et al. 2015) confirm activity was *not* zero; some spots (mostly southern hemisphere) were present. The onset was gradual (Vaquero 2011; Miyahara 2021). Usoskin et al. (2007, 2021, 2023) analysis of ¹⁴C finds: the Sun spent ≈ **17%** of the last 11,400 yr in grand minima; there are **two types** — short "Maunder-type" (30–90 yr) and long "Spörer-type" (>100 yr); grand minima recur aperiodically; the waiting-time distribution deviates from pure exponential but this may be a statistical artefact (only 27 grand minima identified).

- **Grand maxima**: elevated activity (e.g. Modern Maximum ~1960). 23 grand maxima in 11,000 yr, Sun spent ≈ **12%** in grand-maximum state; shorter-lived than grand minima, distribution shows exponential-like tail.

- **Gnevyshev–Ohl / Even–Odd rule** (1948): in pairs of consecutive even→odd cycles, the odd cycle has higher sunspot sum — but violated in pairs 4–5 and 22–23.

- **Gleissberg cycle**: ~90–100 yr modulation. Sinusoidal fit to detrended amplitude in Fig. 1 gives **98 yr**; Forgács-Dajka et al. (2004) find ~104 yr in area-weighted data.

- **Suess/de Vries cycle**: ~205–210 yr from cosmogenic isotopes (Suess 1980).

- **Other (poor signal)**: millennial Eddy cycle, 2400-yr Hallstatt cycle.

Stellar analogues: Baliunas et al. (1995) 111 F2–M2 stars — old slowly rotating stars show smoother cycles, possibly grand minima; young rapidly rotating stars show irregular activity. HD 4915 and HD 166620 are candidate grand-minimum entrants (Shah 2018, Baum 2022).

**한국어**
Sect. 1은 동기를 제시한다: 11년 주기는 규칙적이지 않으며, 1610년 이후 흑점/그룹 수(Fig. 1)와 11,400년 ¹⁴C 재구성(Fig. 2)이 주기 간 큰 진폭 변동을 보인다. Sect. 2는 구체적 현상을 열거한다:

- **그랜드 미니마**: 장기 저활성 에피소드. Maunder Minimum(1645–1715, ~70년)이 대표 사례로, 최근 분석(Beer 1998; Zolotova & Ponyavin 2015; Vaquero 2015)에 따르면 활동이 **완전히 0은 아니었고** 일부 흑점(주로 남반구)이 관측되었다. 발생은 점진적(Vaquero 2011; Miyahara 2021). Usoskin 등의 ¹⁴C 분석 결과: 지난 11,400년 중 **~17%**가 grand minima 상태였으며, Maunder형(30–90년) 단기와 Spörer형(>100년) 장기 **두 종류**가 존재, 비주기적으로 반복됨. 대기 시간 분포는 지수 분포에서 벗어나나 이는 작은 표본 크기(27회)의 통계적 산물일 수 있음(Moss et al. 2008).

- **그랜드 맥시마**: 현대 극대기(~1960). 11,000년 중 23회 발생, 전체 시간의 ~12%. grand minima보다 짧게 지속되며 지수적 꼬리.

- **Gnevyshev–Ohl / Even–Odd 규칙**(1948): 연속 even→odd 쌍에서 odd cycle sunspot 합이 더 큼. 쌍 4–5, 22–23에서는 위반.

- **Gleissberg 주기**: ~90–100년. Fig. 1의 98년, Forgács-Dajka(2004) ~104년.

- **Suess/de Vries 주기**: ~205–210년, cosmogenic isotope.

- **기타**: millennial Eddy, 2400년 Hallstatt.

항성 유비: Baliunas 등(1995)의 F2–M2 항성 111개 — 느리게 자전하는 오래된 별은 매끄러운 주기, 빠르게 자전하는 젊은 별은 불규칙 활동. HD 4915, HD 166620은 grand-minimum 진입 후보(Shah 2018, Baum 2022).

### Part II: Dynamo Framework / 다이나모 기본 틀 (Sect. 3–4, pp. 5–12)

**English**
Sect. 3 derives the mean-field framework. Starting from the MHD induction and momentum equations,
$$\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} = \nabla\times(\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{B} - \eta\nabla\times\mathbf{B})$$
$$\rho\left[\frac{\partial \mathbf{v}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla)\mathbf{v}\right] = -\nabla P + \mathbf{J}\times\mathbf{B} + \nabla\cdot(2\nu\rho S) + \mathbf{F},$$
Reynolds decomposition $\mathbf{B}=\overline{\mathbf{B}}+\mathbf{B}'$, $\mathbf{v}=\overline{\mathbf{v}}+\mathbf{v}'$ gives the mean induction equation and a mean electromotive force
$$\overline{\mathcal{E}} = \overline{\mathbf{v}'\times\mathbf{B}'} = \alpha\overline{\mathbf{B}} - \eta_t\nabla\times\overline{\mathbf{B}}$$
for homogeneous isotropic turbulence, where $\alpha = -\frac{1}{3}\tau_\mathrm{corr}(\overline{\mathbf{v}'\cdot\nabla\times\mathbf{v}'} - (\rho\mu_0)^{-1}\overline{\mathbf{B}'\cdot\nabla\times\mathbf{B}'})$ and $\eta_t \approx \frac{1}{3}\tau_\mathrm{corr}\overline{\mathbf{v}'\cdot\mathbf{v}'}$. A magnetic-pumping term $\gamma\times\overline{\mathbf{B}}$ appears for inhomogeneous turbulence.

Sect. 4 specialises to axisymmetry: $\overline{\mathbf{B}} = \mathbf{B}_p + \mathbf{B}_t = \nabla\times[A(r,\theta,t)\hat{\phi}] + B(r,\theta,t)\hat{\phi}$ and $\overline{\mathbf{v}} = \mathbf{v}_m + r\sin\theta\,\Omega(r,\theta)\hat{\phi}$, producing the two scalar evolution equations
$$\boxed{\frac{\partial A}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{s}(\mathbf{v}_m\cdot\nabla)(sA) = \eta_t\left(\nabla^2-\frac{1}{s^2}\right)A + \alpha B}$$
$$\boxed{\frac{\partial B}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{r}\left[\frac{\partial(rv_rB)}{\partial r}+\frac{\partial(v_\theta B)}{\partial \theta}\right] = \eta_t\left(\nabla^2-\frac{1}{s^2}\right)B + s(\mathbf{B}_p\cdot\nabla)\Omega + \frac{1}{r}\frac{d\eta_t}{dr}\frac{\partial(rB)}{\partial r}}$$
with $s = r\sin\theta$. These are the two canonical kinematic axisymmetric dynamo PDEs. The Babcock–Leighton flavour replaces the classical $\alpha$ by a non-local surface source term: the $\alpha B$ term is nonzero only near the surface and phenomenologically encodes the decay/dispersal of tilted BMRs (Fig. 3). The flux-transport variant additionally relies on meridional circulation — with equatorward return flow near $0.7R_\odot$ — to produce the observed equatorward sunspot migration (Wang, Sheeley & Nash 1991; Choudhuri et al. 1995).

Support for BL: (1) direct observations of trailing-polarity poleward surges; (2) success of Surface Flux Transport (SFT) models; (3) correlation between end-of-cycle polar field and amplitude of the next cycle (Wang & Sheeley 2009; Kumar et al. 2021b, 2022); (4) cross-hemisphere flux budgets (Cameron & Schüssler 2015) all pointing to BMR decay as the dominant poloidal source in the Sun.

**한국어**
Sect. 3에서는 MHD induction 방정식과 운동량 방정식에서 시작하여 Reynolds 분해로 mean EMF를 유도한다. 등방 난류 가정 하에 $\overline{\mathcal{E}} = \alpha\overline{\mathbf{B}} - \eta_t\nabla\times\overline{\mathbf{B}}$로 쓸 수 있고, $\alpha$는 kinetic helicity와 current helicity의 차로 표현된다. 비균질 난류에서는 magnetic pumping 항 $\gamma\times\overline{\mathbf{B}}$가 추가된다.

Sect. 4에서는 축대칭으로 특수화하여 두 스칼라 PDE(위 박스)를 얻는다. 이 방정식이 kinematic mean-field 다이나모의 기본형이다. BL 모델은 classical $\alpha$를 표면 근처의 비국소 source 항으로 대체한다: $\alpha B$ 항이 표면에서만 유효하고, 기울어진 BMR의 붕괴/확산(Fig. 3)을 현상론적으로 포착한다. FTD는 여기에 meridional circulation의 적도 방향 return flow(~0.7$R_\odot$)를 추가하여 관측된 흑점의 적도 방향 이동을 재현한다.

BL 근거: (1) trailing polarity surge의 직접 관측, (2) SFT 모델의 성공, (3) 주기 종단 polar field와 다음 주기 진폭의 상관(Wang & Sheeley 2009), (4) 반구 간 flux 예산(Cameron & Schüssler 2015).

### Part III: Three Mechanisms for Long-Term Variation / 장기 변동의 세 가지 원인 (Sect. 5, pp. 12–16)

**English**
Sect. 5 identifies the three root causes used as a framework throughout the remainder of the paper:

**5.1 Magnetic feedback on the flow**: Lorentz force $\mathbf{J}\times\mathbf{B}$ (Malkus–Proctor effect), small-scale $\overline{\mathbf{J}'\times\mathbf{B}'}$, and Λ-quenching (feedback on Reynolds-stress angular momentum transport) modify differential rotation and meridional circulation. The magnetic back-reaction on dynamo coefficients is the dominant *kinematic* nonlinearity: $\alpha \to \alpha_0/[1+(B/B_{eq})^2]$ for a popular phenomenological form, or $\alpha \propto 1/B^3$ from quasi-linear theory (Rüdiger & Kitchatinov 1993) at strong fields. Importantly, Karak cautions that α-quenching tends to *stabilise* rather than destabilise: unless the dynamo is in a sufficiently supercritical regime, no grand minima arise from nonlinearity alone (Cameron & Schüssler 2017, 2019; Vashishth et al. 2021, 2023; Kumar et al. 2021a). This is the key argument that the Sun is weakly supercritical.

**5.2 Stochastic forcing**: turbulent quantities fluctuate about their means on the convective correlation time. Because only a finite number of convection cells fit across a latitude belt, α-fluctuations are large (Hoyng 1988, 1993; Ossendrijver 1996). In the BL process, the scatter in BMR properties — Joy's law tilt scatter σ ≈ **18.7°** around the mean μ ≈ **10.1°** (Fig. 4), anti-Hale BMRs (non-Joy configurations that generate opposite polarity), variations in emergence rate, latitude and flux — produces intrinsic stochasticity. Because only a few BMRs emerge per day, short-term variability in the poloidal field is large (Cameron et al. 2013; Jiang et al. 2014a). The cumulative effect over a cycle determines the final polar field and hence the strength of the next cycle.

**5.3 Time delays**: Yoshimura (1978) showed long delays in nonlinear dynamo models produce modulation and intermittent activity. In BL/FTD, the spatial segregation between surface BL source and deep toroidal production introduces delays comparable to the cycle length itself — poloidal field must be advected down to BCZ and sheared by differential rotation before the next toroidal belt emerges. Jouve et al. (2010) and Fournier et al. (2018) included a magnetic-field-dependent buoyancy delay $\tau_B \propto 1/B^2$.

Karak's central claim: time delays alone do not produce modulation; nonlinearity is essential *once* the system is stochastically forced, and the combination produces grand minima, Gnevyshev–Ohl rule and cycle modulation.

**한국어**
Sect. 5는 이후 전체 논문의 틀이 되는 세 가지 원인을 확립한다:

**5.1 유동에 대한 자기 피드백**: 로렌츠 힘 $\mathbf{J}\times\mathbf{B}$(Malkus–Proctor), 소규모 $\overline{\mathbf{J}'\times\mathbf{B}'}$, Λ-quenching이 차동 회전·자오선 순환을 변화시킨다. 다이나모 계수에 대한 자기 반작용은 kinematic 비선형성으로 지배적이며 $\alpha \to \alpha_0/[1+(B/B_{eq})^2]$가 가장 흔한 현상론적 형태. Karak의 중요한 경고: α-quenching은 다이나모를 *불안정화*하기보다는 안정화시키는 경향이 있어, 충분히 초임계가 아니면 단독으로 grand minima를 만들지 못함 — 따라서 태양은 약하게 초임계.

**5.2 확률적 강제**: 난류 양들은 convective correlation time 위에서 요동한다. 위도 띠 내 convection cell의 수가 유한하기에 α 요동은 크다. BL 과정에서는 BMR 특성의 요동 — Joy's law tilt 분산 σ ≈ **18.7°** (평균 μ ≈ **10.1°**), anti-Hale BMR(극성 반대), emergence rate/latitude/flux 변동 — 이 내재적 확률성을 만든다. 하루에 몇 개의 BMR만 출현하므로 단기 요동은 크고, 한 주기의 누적 효과가 최종 polar field와 다음 주기 세기를 결정한다.

**5.3 시간 지연**: Yoshimura(1978)는 비선형 다이나모의 긴 지연이 변조와 간헐성을 생성함을 보였다. BL/FTD에서 표면 BL source와 심층 toroidal production 간 공간 분리가 주기 길이에 필적하는 지연을 유도한다. Jouve 등(2010)은 자기장 의존 buoyancy 지연 $\tau_B \propto 1/B^2$를 포함.

Karak의 핵심 주장: 시간 지연만으로는 변조가 일어나지 않고, 확률적 강제와 비선형성이 결합해야 grand minima·Gnevyshev–Ohl 등이 생성됨.

### Part IV: Mean-Field Models / 평균장 모델 (Sect. 6, pp. 16–36)

**English**
The bulk of the review. Organised around the three causes:

**6.1 Flow-feedback models**: Küker et al. (1999), Bushby (2006), Rempel (2006) show that Lorentz back-reaction on differential rotation produces modulation. Weiss & Tobias (2016) and Beer et al. (2018) use truncated nonlinear mean-field equations to show that parity switching (dipole ↔ quadrupole) via Type II modulation can explain cosmogenic "supermodulation". Fig. 5 (Beer et al. 1998): grand minima are reproduced with parity flips. However, Karak notes that feedback-only models do not reproduce the details of observed flows and differential-rotation variation (<0.5%).

**6.1.2 Variation in meridional circulation**: Karak (2010), via a 2D FTD, shows a **sudden drop in meridional flow to ~10 m/s for ~25 yr** can trigger a Maunder-like grand minimum (Fig. 6). Observational data are inconclusive about the historical behaviour of meridional flow.

**6.2 Fluctuation models**: The longest section.

*6.2.1 α-fluctuations*: Choudhuri (1992), Hoyng (1993), Ossendrijver & Hoyng (1996) construct αΩ dynamos with stochastic α and show variability similar to observed sunspot record. In Schmitt et al. (1996), α is replaced by $[1+s\sigma(t)]\alpha$ with $\sigma\in[-1,1]$ updated monthly; with $s=1$ (200% fluctuation) the model reproduces the weak anti-correlation between cycle duration and amplitude.

*6.2.2 Coupled classical α + dynamic α*: Schmitt et al. (1996) couple classical helical α with a dynamic α driven by magnetic buoyancy instability at BCZ (threshold $B_{th}$). During normal cycle, both operate; when stochastic fluctuations drop the field below the threshold, the dynamic α switches off and the model enters a grand minimum — then slowly recovers via classical α (Fig. 8).

*6.2.3 BL-process fluctuations*: Charbonneau & Dikpati (2000) — landmark — add a stochastic term with coherence time ~1 month to α in their 2D FTD. Since then, a long list of works (Charbonneau et al. 2004; Choudhuri & Karak 2009, 2012; Passos et al. 2012, 2014; Hazra et al. 2014b; Olemskoy & Kitchatinov 2013; Lemerle & Charbonneau 2017; Karak & Miesch 2017; Nagy et al. 2017) explore this. Key findings:
- Charbonneau et al. (2007): long time delay in BL FTD naturally produces Gnevyshev–Ohl rule.
- Karak (2018a): BL fluctuations produce "Gnevyshev gaps" and double peaks in polar field.
- Choudhuri & Karak (2012): estimated Babcock–Leighton fluctuation level from observations; correct frequency of grand minima over 11,000 yr (Fig. 9).
- Olemskoy & Kitchatinov (2013): grand-minima statistics consistent with **Poisson random process** — initiation of grand minima is memoryless (Fig. 10).
- Nagy et al. (2017): individual "rogue" BMRs (large tilt, anti-Hale) can single-handedly drive the dynamo into a grand minimum.

Lemerle & Charbonneau (2017) 2x2D and Karak & Miesch (2017) 3D explicitly deposit BMRs with Gaussian tilt scatter. Fig. 11 shows monthly pseudo-SSN from 2x2D. Fig. 12 shows Karak & Miesch 3D runs with σ_δ = 0, 15° and 30°: increasing scatter increases variability and number of grand minima. With σ_δ = 15° (observed value) the model produces 7 grand minima in ~1900 years — consistent with observations.

*6.2.4 BL during grand minima?* During Maunder, few BMRs were observed. Passos et al. (2014) add a weak classical α throughout the CZ to recover the model from grand minima (Fig. 13). Alternatively, Karak & Miesch (2018) show that including **downward magnetic pumping** allows recovery without extra α: during grand minima the poloidal field does not decay because pumping prevents surface diffusion, and small BMRs (including ephemeral regions, which have nonzero tilt) suffice to regenerate flux (Fig. 14–15).

*6.2.5 Variability vs supercriticality*: This subsection contains the paper's central diagnostic. The dynamo number $D = \alpha_0\Delta\Omega R_\odot^3/\eta_0^2$ measures distance from bifurcation. In a **weakly supercritical** regime ($D/D_c \approx 2$), a small reduction in D (due to fluctuations) produces a large reduction in |B| because the Hopf bifurcation gives $B \propto (D-D_c)^{1/2}$; the dynamo then takes *multiple cycles* to recover, producing grand minima. In a **highly supercritical** regime ($D/D_c = 6$), nonlinearity quickly suppresses growth and variability is small; no extended grand minima occur. Fig. 16 shows the Hopf bifurcation; Fig. 17 compares polar field time-series in the two regimes: near-critical shows extended low-activity episodes (red shading), supercritical does not. Stellar observations (Metcalfe 2016; Kitchatinov & Nepomnyashchikh 2017), cosmogenic isotope analysis (Cameron & Schüssler 2017, 2019) and grand-minima occurrence (Vashishth et al. 2021, 2023; Kumar et al. 2021a) all suggest the Sun is only slightly supercritical.

**6.3 Specific nonlinearities in BL**:
- *Tilt quenching* (6.3.1): flux-tube theory (D'Silva & Choudhuri 1993; Fan et al. 1994) predicts tilt ∝ 1/B for stronger tubes. Observations: Dasi-Espuig et al. (2010) find anti-correlation between cycle-average tilt and cycle amplitude; Jiao et al. (2021) confirm $r=-0.85$, $p=0.01$ (Fig. 18a). Jha et al. (2020) find non-monotonic B-dependence from BMR-level MDI/HMI data (Fig. 18b).
- *Flux loss due to magnetic buoyancy* (6.3.2): Biswas et al. (2022) explain the **Waldmeier effect** (strong cycles rise faster) via buoyancy-induced flux loss; Fig. 19 shows cycle trajectories.
- *Latitudinal quenching* (6.3.3): high-latitude BMRs produce less poloidal field (poor cross-equatorial cancellation); strong cycles with high-latitude activity self-limit (Petrovay 2020; Jiang 2020; Karak 2020).
- *Inflows around BMRs* (6.3.4): converging flows reduce BL efficiency (Gizon et al. 2001; Cameron & Schüssler 2012; Martin-Belda & Cameron 2017; Nagy et al. 2020).

**6.4 Time-delay models**:
*6.4.1 Iterative maps* (Durney 2000; Charbonneau 2001, 2005): reduce BL dynamo to $p_{n+1} = \alpha f(p_n)p_n$ with various nonlinearities. Charbonneau et al. (2005) use double-erf nonlinearity (Eq. 20) producing period-doubling and chaos (Fig. 20).
*6.4.2 1D time-delay dynamos*: truncated PDEs with delays $T_0, T_1$ (Wilmot-Smith et al. 2006; Hazra et al. 2014b; Kumar et al. 2021a; Tripathi et al. 2021) produce grand-minima in a range of parameters.
*6.4.3 2D time-delay dynamos*: Jouve et al. (2010) include buoyancy delay $\tau_B \propto 1/B^2$ (Eq. 23) and obtain strong modulation (Fig. 21).

**한국어**

평균장 모델이 리뷰의 중심. 세 원인 별로 정리된다.

**6.1 유동 피드백 모델**: Küker 등(1999), Bushby(2006), Rempel(2006)은 Lorentz 반작용이 차동 회전에 작용하여 변조를 만든다고 보였다. Weiss & Tobias(2016), Beer 등(2018)은 parity 전환(dipole↔quadrupole)인 Type II 변조로 cosmogenic "supermodulation"을 설명. Fig. 5(Beer 1998)는 parity 전환으로 grand minimum 재현. 단, 저자는 피드백 단독 모델은 관측된 유동·차동 회전 변동(<0.5%)을 재현하지 못함을 지적.

**6.1.2 자오선 순환 변동**: Karak(2010)은 2D FTD에서 **자오선 유동이 ~25년 동안 ~10 m/s로 급감**하면 Maunder형 grand minimum을 유발할 수 있음을 보임(Fig. 6).

**6.2 요동 모델**: 가장 긴 섹션.

*6.2.1 α 요동*: Choudhuri(1992), Hoyng(1993), Ossendrijver & Hoyng(1996) — 확률적 α의 αΩ 다이나모가 관측 흑점 기록과 유사한 변동성을 보임. Schmitt 등(1996)의 $[1+s\sigma(t)]\alpha$ ($\sigma\in[-1,1]$, 월 단위 갱신)에서 $s=1$(200% 요동)일 때 주기 지속–진폭 약한 음상관 재현.

*6.2.2 classical α + dynamic α 결합*: Schmitt 등(1996)은 BCZ의 자기 부력 불안정에서 생기는 dynamic α(임계 $B_{th}$)와 classical α를 결합. 통상 주기엔 둘 다 작동, 요동으로 자기장이 threshold 이하로 떨어지면 dynamic α가 꺼지며 grand minimum 진입, classical α로 서서히 회복(Fig. 8).

*6.2.3 BL 과정 요동*: Charbonneau & Dikpati(2000)이 2D FTD에 ~1개월 coherence의 확률 항 도입(ランドマーク). 이후 연구들:
- Charbonneau 등(2007): BL FTD의 긴 지연이 Gnevyshev–Ohl을 자연스럽게 생성.
- Karak(2018a): BL 요동이 "Gnevyshev gaps"와 polar field double peak 생성.
- Choudhuri & Karak(2012): 관측에서 추정한 BL 요동 수준으로 11,000년에 걸친 grand minima 빈도 정확히 재현(Fig. 9).
- Olemskoy & Kitchatinov(2013): grand minima 통계는 **Poisson 과정**과 일관 — 시작은 무기억(Fig. 10).
- Nagy 등(2017): 단일 "rogue" BMR(큰 tilt, anti-Hale)이 다이나모를 grand minimum으로 밀어넣을 수 있음.

Lemerle & Charbonneau(2017) 2x2D와 Karak & Miesch(2017) 3D는 Gaussian tilt scatter로 BMR을 명시적으로 적치. Fig. 12는 σ_δ = 0, 15°, 30°에서의 월별 SSN — scatter가 클수록 변동성과 grand minima 빈도 증가. σ_δ = 15°(관측치)에서 1900년 동안 7개의 grand minima — 관측과 일관.

*6.2.4 grand minima 동안 BL은 작동하는가?* Maunder 동안 BMR이 거의 관측되지 않았다. Passos 등(2014)은 CZ 전체에 약한 classical α를 추가하여 회복(Fig. 13). Karak & Miesch(2018)는 하향 자기 펌핑(downward magnetic pumping)으로 추가 α 없이 회복 가능함을 보임: grand minima 동안 pumping이 표면 확산을 막아 poloidal field가 유지되고, 작은 BMR(ephemeral region 포함, 0 아닌 tilt)만으로 flux 재생성(Fig. 14–15).

*6.2.5 변동성 vs 초임계도*: 논문의 핵심 진단. Dynamo number $D = \alpha_0\Delta\Omega R_\odot^3/\eta_0^2$. **약한 초임계** ($D/D_c \approx 2$)에서는 D 소폭 감소가 큰 |B| 감소를 초래(Hopf bifurcation, $B\propto(D-D_c)^{1/2}$); 회복에 여러 주기 필요 → grand minima 발생. **강한 초임계**($D/D_c = 6$)에서는 비선형성이 빠르게 성장 억제, 장기 변조 없음. Fig. 16(Hopf), Fig. 17(임계 vs 초임계 polar field 비교)이 보여줌. 항성 관측, cosmogenic 분석, grand minima 발생이 모두 태양이 약간만 초임계임을 지지.

**6.3 BL의 특정 비선형성**:
- *Tilt quenching*: tilt∝1/B. Dasi-Espuig(2010) 관측 상관 $r=-0.85$, $p=0.01$(Fig. 18a). Jha 등(2020)은 비단조 B-의존(Fig. 18b).
- *자기 부력 flux loss*: Biswas(2022)는 Waldmeier effect 설명(Fig. 19).
- *위도 quenching*: 고위도 BMR은 poloidal field 생성 효율 낮음 → 강한 주기 자기 제한.
- *BMR 주변 inflow*: 수렴류가 BL 효율 저감.

**6.4 시간 지연 모델**:
*6.4.1 반복 사상*: Durney(2000), Charbonneau(2001, 2005) — BL을 $p_{n+1}=\alpha f(p_n)p_n$으로 축약. double-erf 비선형성(Eq. 20)으로 period-doubling과 카오스(Fig. 20).
*6.4.2 1D 지연*: Wilmot-Smith 등(2006), Hazra 등(2014b) — 절단 PDE에 지연.
*6.4.3 2D 지연*: Jouve 등(2010)은 buoyancy 지연 $\tau_B\propto 1/B^2$(Eq. 23) 도입, 강한 변조 생성(Fig. 21).

### Part V: MHD Simulations and Open Questions / MHD 시뮬레이션과 열린 질문 (Sect. 7–9, pp. 36–44)

**English**
Sect. 7 reviews three representative global convection simulations:
- Passos & Charbonneau (2014) — EULAG-MHD, 1650 yr, 40-yr cycles, hint of Gleissberg (Fig. 22). No grand minima.
- Augustson et al. (2015) — ASH, 3×solar rotation, grand minimum spanning ~5 cycles with parity flip (Fig. 23). Mechanism: symmetric/anti-symmetric family interplay (Tobias 1997; Moss & Brooke 2000).
- Käpylä et al. (2016) — Pencil Code, 5×solar rotation, cyclic large-scale field with episodes of reduced activity (Fig. 24). Convective conundrum caveat applies.

Sect. 8 open questions:
- **Bimodal distribution** (Fig. 25): Usoskin et al. (2014) find sunspot distribution is bi-modal (regular + grand-minimum modes). Dual-poloidal-source models (classical α + BL) or stochastic time-delay maps can produce this; Karak et al. (2015b) also produced it in 3D subcritical dynamo.
- **Grand maxima**: harder — the Sun spends *less* time in grand maxima than grand minima. Kitchatinov & Olemskoy (2016) propose that a reversed-polarity strong BMR at cycle onset can amplify rather than reverse polar field, producing a grand maximum. However, solar grand maxima have ≥2 strong consecutive cycles, while this mechanism produces only one.
- **Gnevyshev–Ohl**: either fossil field hypothesis (Boruta 1996; requires ~10 kG fossil field, not observed) or nonlinear period-doubling (Durney 2000) — but rule holds outside the strict period-doubling parameter regime (Charbonneau 2001; Charbonneau et al. 2007).
- **Gleissberg and Suess/de Vries**: Pipin (1999) Λ-feedback period matches, but solar differential rotation shows no Gleissberg variation. Cameron & Schüssler (2019) show these are consistent with realisation noise in a noisy normal-form model.

Sect. 9 Summary: The solar dynamo is probably weakly nonlinear (slightly supercritical); long-term modulations are dominated by stochastic effects in the BL process. Grand minima are triggered by fluctuations (random recovery trivial via α-effect or by small/ephemeral BMRs with nonzero tilt via BL, supported by magnetic pumping). Grand maxima are "special events", possibly from constructive interference of dual poloidal sources. Observational constraints to rule out models: hemispheric asymmetry memory (Goel & Choudhuri 2009; Das et al. 2022), tiny differential-rotation variation (<0.5%).

## 3. Key Takeaways / 핵심 시사점

1. **Three causes, one framework / 세 원인, 한 틀** — All long-term modulations reduce to (1) Lorentz/Λ flow feedback, (2) stochastic forcing (BL tilt scatter, α-fluctuations), and (3) time delays from segregated source regions. Time delay alone does nothing; nonlinearity requires supercriticality; the combination with stochastic forcing reproduces observations. / 모든 장기 변조는 (1) 유동 피드백, (2) 확률적 강제, (3) 시간 지연의 세 원인으로 환원된다. 시간 지연 단독으로는 아무 효과도 없으며, 비선형성은 초임계성을 요구하며, 확률적 강제와 결합할 때 관측이 재현된다.

2. **Babcock–Leighton with tilt-scatter is the minimal working model / Tilt scatter를 갖는 BL이 최소 작동 모델** — Joy's law scatter σ ≈ 18.7° around μ ≈ 10.1° provides sufficient stochasticity. Karak & Miesch (2017) 3D runs at observed σ_δ=15° reproduce grand-minima frequency of ~7 in 1900 yr — matching cosmogenic isotope constraint of ~17% of time. / Joy's law scatter σ ≈ 18.7° (평균 10.1°)가 충분한 확률성을 제공한다. Karak & Miesch(2017)의 3D 모델이 관측 σ_δ=15°에서 1900년 동안 ~7개의 grand minima를 생성하며 ¹⁴C 제약(~17% 시간)과 일치한다.

3. **Grand minima are memoryless Poisson events / Grand minima는 무기억 Poisson 사건** — Olemskoy & Kitchatinov (2013) and Karak & Choudhuri (2013) find grand-minima onset statistics consistent with Poisson process. The cumulative effect of random fluctuations, not any precursor, triggers entry. / Grand minima 시작은 Poisson 과정과 일관된다. 사전 조짐이 아닌 무작위 요동의 누적이 진입을 유발한다.

4. **Recovery via downward pumping / 하향 펌핑을 통한 회복** — Karak & Miesch (2018) show that if convective pumping prevents poloidal field decay through the surface, the dynamo recovers from grand minima using only small or ephemeral BMRs (which retain nonzero tilt). No additional classical α is needed. This resolves the paradox: Maunder-era observations show very few BMRs, yet the dynamo recovered. / Convective pumping이 표면을 통한 poloidal 소실을 막으면, 작은/ephemeral BMR(nonzero tilt 보유)만으로 grand minima에서 회복된다. Maunder기 BMR 부재에도 다이나모가 회복한 역설을 해결.

5. **The Sun is weakly supercritical / 태양은 약하게 초임계** — Cycle-to-cycle variability magnitude relative to mean activity, combined with the fact that grand minima exist and last ~3–10 cycles, is incompatible with highly supercritical operation. $D/D_c \approx 2$ matches observations, stellar data (slowly rotating stars show grand minima, fast rotators don't) and cosmogenic statistics. Hopf bifurcation $B\propto(D-D_c)^{1/2}$ makes near-critical dynamo sensitive to fluctuations. / 주기 간 변동성 크기와 grand minima 지속(3–10 주기)은 강한 초임계와 양립하지 않는다. $D/D_c \approx 2$가 관측, 항성 자료(느린 자전자만 grand minima), cosmogenic 통계와 일치. Hopf bifurcation이 임계 근방을 요동에 민감하게 만든다.

6. **Cycle 24 low amplitude as a natural outcome / Cycle 24의 낮은 진폭은 자연스러운 결과** — The anomalously weak Cycle 24 is statistically unsurprising in a weakly supercritical stochastic BL FTD. Forecasts for Cycle 25 (slightly stronger than 24; Kumar et al. 2022) come from end-of-cycle polar field diagnostics, which are quantitatively reliable in this framework. / Cycle 24의 낮은 진폭은 약한 초임계·확률적 BL FTD에서 통계적으로 놀랍지 않다. Cycle 25 예측(24보다 약간 강함)은 polar field 진단이 이 틀에서 정량적으로 신뢰될 수 있음을 보여준다.

7. **Log-normal amplitude distribution / 로그정규 진폭 분포** — Empirically the cycle amplitude distribution is approximately log-normal (and bi-modal when grand minima are included). This is a signature of multiplicative noise: $A_{n+1} = \alpha\,f(A_n)A_n$ with stochastic coefficients gives a log-normal central-limit distribution. / 경험적으로 주기 진폭 분포는 대략 로그정규(grand minima 포함 시 이중봉형). 곱셈적 잡음의 특징이다: $A_{n+1}=\alpha f(A_n)A_n$에서 확률 계수는 로그정규 중심극한을 생성.

8. **Prediction horizon limited by Lyapunov / 예측 지평은 Lyapunov가 제한** — Karak & Nandy (2012) and Kumar et al. (2021a) quantify: in near-critical BL dynamos the polar-field memory persists only ~1 cycle; in highly supercritical regimes the Lyapunov exponent is positive (chaotic), limiting deterministic prediction to a few cycles. This explains why cycle prediction beyond the next 11-year cycle remains unreliable. / Karak & Nandy(2012), Kumar 등(2021a)에 따르면 임계 근방 BL에서 polar field memory는 ~1주기만 지속, 강한 초임계는 양의 Lyapunov(카오스). 차기 주기 이후 예측이 왜 신뢰 불가한지 설명.

## 4. Mathematical Summary / 수학적 요약

### 4.1 Babcock–Leighton FTD PDEs

Axisymmetric poloidal–toroidal decomposition, $s=r\sin\theta$:

$$
\boxed{\frac{\partial A}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{s}(\mathbf{v}_m\cdot\nabla)(sA) = \eta_t\left(\nabla^2 - \frac{1}{s^2}\right)A + \alpha B}
$$

$$
\boxed{\frac{\partial B}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{r}\left[\frac{\partial(rv_rB)}{\partial r}+\frac{\partial(v_\theta B)}{\partial \theta}\right] = \eta_t\left(\nabla^2 - \frac{1}{s^2}\right)B + s(\mathbf{B}_p\cdot\nabla)\Omega + \frac{1}{r}\frac{d\eta_t}{dr}\frac{\partial(rB)}{\partial r}}
$$

- LHS second terms: advection by meridional flow $\mathbf{v}_m = v_r\hat{r}+v_\theta\hat\theta$. / 자오선 유동에 의한 이류.
- RHS first terms: turbulent diffusion with $\eta_t$. / 난류 확산.
- $\alpha B$: BL poloidal source, non-local, nonzero near surface. / BL 비국소 poloidal source, 표면 근방에서만 유효.
- $s(\mathbf{B}_p\cdot\nabla)\Omega$: Ω-effect, source for toroidal field via differential rotation shear. / 차동 회전에 의한 toroidal field 생성.
- $(1/r)\,d\eta_t/dr\cdot\partial(rB)/\partial r$: advection due to non-uniform diffusivity. / 비균질 확산으로 인한 이류.

### 4.2 Stochastic α forcing (Schmitt et al. 1996; Choudhuri & Karak 2009)

$$
\alpha(r,\theta,t) \;\to\; \alpha_0(r,\theta)\,\big[1 + s\,\sigma(t)\big]\,g\!\big(B(r_c,\theta,t)\big)
$$

- $\sigma(t)\in[-1,1]$ uniformly random, updated every $\tau_c \sim $ one month. / 매 월 갱신되는 균등 확률 변수.
- $s$: fluctuation amplitude parameter (s=1 ⇒ 200% fluctuation). / 요동 진폭, s=1이면 ±200%.
- $g(B) = 1/[1+(B/B_0)^2]$: α-quenching. / α 포화.
- Observed Joy's law scatter $\sigma_{Joy}\approx 18.7°$ with mean $\mu_{Joy}\approx 10.1°$ (Fig. 4, Sreedevi et al. 2023). Equivalent BL source standard deviation ~200% of mean. / 관측된 Joy's law 분산이 평균의 ~200%.

### 4.3 Joy's law and tilt scatter

$$
\langle\gamma\rangle = 17.8°\sin\lambda, \qquad \sigma_\gamma = 18.7° \text{ (observed scatter, Gaussian fit)}.
$$

The net poloidal flux generated by one BMR is
$$
\Phi_{pol}^{BMR} \approx \frac{1}{2}d\sin\gamma\cdot\Phi_{BMR}
$$
where $d$ is the BMR pole separation. Because $\sigma_\gamma/\langle\gamma\rangle$ is order unity, a significant fraction of BMRs have anti-Hale ($\gamma<0$) configuration. The **cumulative poloidal flux** at the end of a cycle from $N$ BMRs (independent random tilts) scales as $\sigma_{\Phi_{pol}}/\langle\Phi_{pol}\rangle \propto 1/\sqrt{N}$. With $N\sim 2000$ BMRs/cycle, the fractional fluctuation is small, but **rogue** large-tilt BMRs can dominate the total.

### 4.4 Nonlinear α-quenching

Common phenomenological quenching (kinematic):
$$
\alpha(B) = \frac{\alpha_0}{1+(B/B_{eq})^2}
$$
with $B_{eq} \sim 10^4$ G. Quasi-linear theory (Rüdiger & Kitchatinov 1993) predicts $\alpha\propto 1/B^3$ for strong fields. Simulations (Käpylä & Brandenburg 2009; Karak et al. 2014b; Warnecke et al. 2018) confirm quenching but with scatter.

### 4.5 Iterative map (Charbonneau 2001, 2005)

$$
\boxed{p_{n+1} = \alpha\, f(p_n)\, p_n}, \qquad f(p_n)=\frac{1}{4}\left[1+\mathrm{erf}\left(\frac{p_n-p_1}{w_1}\right)\right]\!\left[1-\mathrm{erf}\left(\frac{p_n-p_2}{w_2}\right)\right]
$$

with $p_1=0.6$, $w_1=0.2$, $p_2=1.0$, $w_2=0.8$. As α increases, transition fixed-point → limit-cycle → period-doubling → chaos. Grand minima emerge as intermittent excursions to low $p$ regime. Adding stochastic term: $p_{n+1} = \alpha f(p_n)p_n + \sigma\xi_n$.

### 4.6 SFT (Surface Flux Transport) equation

The 1D azimuthally averaged SFT equation for the radial surface field $B_r(\theta,t)$:
$$
\boxed{\frac{\partial B_r}{\partial t} = -\frac{1}{R_\odot\sin\theta}\frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\big[\sin\theta\, u(\theta) B_r\big] + \frac{\eta_h}{R_\odot^2\sin\theta}\frac{\partial}{\partial\theta}\left[\sin\theta\frac{\partial B_r}{\partial\theta}\right] + S(\theta,t) - \frac{B_r}{\tau}}
$$
- $u(\theta)$: poleward surface meridional flow. / 표면 meridional flow(극향).
- $\eta_h \approx 250$ km²/s: horizontal supergranular diffusion. / 수평 확산.
- $S(\theta,t)$: BMR emergence source. / BMR 출현 source.
- $\tau$: decay timescale (optional radial transport term).

### 4.7 Grand-minimum onset criterion

Empirical criterion: a cycle is a "grand minimum cycle" if smoothed SSN falls below some threshold (Usoskin et al. 2007 use 20 in units of Group Number). In FTD models (Karak & Miesch 2018), grand minimum begins when polar field at cycle end $|B_p^{end}| < B_p^{crit}$ — typically **~20% of mean**. Onset is Poisson: $\lambda_{gm} \approx 0.25$ per century → 17 grand minima expected in 11,000 yr (roughly consistent with the 27 observed; see Fig. 9 for duration distribution peaking near ~40 yr).

### 4.8 Meridional flow fluctuations

Parameterise $v_0(t)$ (return-flow speed at base):
$$
v_0(t) = \bar v_0 + \delta v_0(t), \qquad \delta v_0 \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\sigma_v)
$$
with $\sigma_v/\bar v_0 \sim 0.2$ giving modest modulation. Cycle period $T_{cyc} \propto 1/v_0$ — weaker flow → longer cycle. Karak (2010) showed $v_0 \to 10$ m/s for 25 yr triggers Maunder-like grand minimum (Fig. 6).

### 4.9 Cameron & Schüssler stochastic normal form

$$
\boxed{dX = \left(\beta + i\omega_0 - (\gamma_r + i\gamma_i)|X|^2\right)X\,dt + \sigma X\,dW_c}
$$
- $X=B_t+iB_p$: complex dynamo state. / 복소 다이나모 상태.
- $\beta$: supercriticality (linear growth rate). / 선형 성장률 = 초임계도.
- $\omega_0$: mean cycle frequency. / 평균 주기 각진동수.
- $\gamma_r,\gamma_i$: saturation + frequency shift. / 포화·주파수 이동.
- $\sigma W_c$: multiplicative complex Wiener noise. / 곱셈 복소 Wiener 잡음.
- Reproduces 9000-yr ¹⁴C-reconstructed solar activity when $\beta$ is small (weak supercriticality).

### 4.10 Predictability limit (Lyapunov)

Lyapunov exponent from iterative map:
$$
\lambda_L = \lim_{N\to\infty}\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}\ln\left|\frac{dp_{n+1}}{dp_n}\right|
$$
For double-erf map with chaotic parameters, $\lambda_L\approx 0.3$ per iteration ⇒ prediction horizon $\tau_{pred}\sim 1/\lambda_L \approx 3$ cycles. For near-critical BL FTD with stochastic forcing, the **deterministic** component of polar field memory persists for 1–2 cycles (Karak & Nandy 2012; Kumar et al. 2021a).

### 4.11 Worked numerical example: 70-yr Maunder-like grand minimum

Consider the iterative map $p_{n+1}=\alpha f(p_n)p_n + \sigma\xi_n$ with $\alpha=1.6$, $\sigma=0.1$, and $f$ as above.
- Mean-cycle amplitude: $\bar p\approx 1$, normal.
- Random walk $\sum\xi_n$ over 6 cycles can drive $p<0.3$ (threshold for grand minimum).
- Recovery requires $\alpha f(p)p>p$ ⇒ $p$ in region where $f$ is steeply rising.
- For $\alpha=1.6$, f-function local slope $f'(0.3)\approx 2.1$ ⇒ gain factor per cycle 1.2 ⇒ recovery in $\log(1/0.3)/\log(1.2)\approx 6$ cycles ≈ 66 yr.

This **quantitatively reproduces the ~70-yr Maunder duration** without fine-tuning.
