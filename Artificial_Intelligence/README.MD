# Artificial Intelligence

## Overview
A study track covering the full history and progression of Artificial Intelligence — from foundational concepts to modern deep learning and beyond. Learning is driven by reading curated landmark papers and implementing key ideas in code.

## Learning Roadmap

### Phase 1: Foundations (1940s–1980s)
- The birth of AI: Turing, perceptrons, symbolic AI
- Early neural networks and the first AI winter
- Key math prerequisites: linear algebra, calculus, probability

### Phase 2: Classical Machine Learning (1980s–2010s)
- Backpropagation and the revival of neural networks
- SVMs, decision trees, ensemble methods
- Statistical learning theory and generalization

### Phase 3: Deep Learning Revolution (2010s)
- CNNs for computer vision
- RNNs, LSTMs, and sequence modeling
- Optimization, regularization, and training techniques

### Phase 4: Modern AI (2017–Present)
- Attention mechanism and Transformers
- Large Language Models (GPT, BERT, and beyond)
- Generative models (GANs, VAEs, Diffusion)
- Reinforcement Learning and RLHF

### Phase 5: Frontiers
- Multimodal models
- AI alignment and safety
- Emerging architectures and paradigms
