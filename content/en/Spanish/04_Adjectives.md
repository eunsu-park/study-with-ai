## 3. Adjective Agreement Rules

### A. Gender Agreement

Spanish adjectives must agree with the gender (masculine/feminine) of the noun they modify.

#### Adjectives ending in -o/a
- Masculine: **bonito**, **alto**, **nuevo**
- Feminine: **bonita**, **alta**, **nueva**

**Examples:**
- el gato **negro** / the black cat (masculine)
- la gata **negra** / the black cat (feminine)
- el libro **nuevo** / the new book
- la casa **nueva** / the new house

#### Adjectives ending in -e
No gender change
- grande, fuerte, inteligente, interesante

**Examples:**
- el coche **grande** / the big car
- la casa **grande** / the big house

#### Adjectives ending in consonant
Most don't change for gender
- azul, gris, fácil, difícil, joven

**Examples:**
- el libro **azul** / the blue book
- la mesa **azul** / the blue table

**Exception - Nationality adjectives:**
- español / española (Spanish)
- francés / francesa (French)
- alemán / alemana (German)
- inglés / inglesa (English)

### B. Number Agreement

Adjectives must also agree with the number (singular/plural) of the noun.

#### Making Plurals

**Ends in vowel → add -s:**
- bonito → bonitos
- bonita → bonitas
- grande → grandes

**Ends in consonant → add -es:**
- azul → azules
- fácil → fáciles
- joven → jóvenes

**Examples:**
- el gato negro → los gatos **negros**
- la casa nueva → las casas **nuevas**
- el libro azul → los libros **azules**

### C. Complete Adjective Ending Pattern

| Ending | Masculine Singular | Feminine Singular | Masculine Plural | Feminine Plural |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| -o/a | bonito | bonita | bonitos | bonitas |
| -o/a | alto | alta | altos | altas |
| -e | grande | grande | grandes | grandes |
| -e | fuerte | fuerte | fuertes | fuertes |
| Consonant | azul | azul | azules | azules |
| Consonant | fácil | fácil | fáciles | fáciles |

### D. Adjective Placement

#### After Noun (typical)
Most adjectives come after the noun
- un libro **interesante** / an interesting book
- una casa **grande** / a big house
- un coche **rojo** / a red car

#### Before Noun (limited)
Some adjectives change meaning when placed before the noun

**grande:**
- un **gran** hombre / a great man
- un hombre **grande** / a big man

**pobre:**
- un **pobre** hombre / a poor (pitiful) man
- un hombre **pobre** / a poor (not rich) man

**viejo:**
- un **viejo** amigo / an old (long-time) friend
- un amigo **viejo** / an old (aged) friend

#### Always Before Noun
- **bueno/a** (good) - buen libro
- **malo/a** (bad) - mal día
- **mucho/a** (much) - mucha gente
- **poco/a** (little) - poco tiempo

**Note:** bueno and malo shorten to buen, mal before masculine singular nouns
- un **buen** libro / a good book
- un **mal** día / a bad day

### E. Irregular Comparatives/Superlatives

Some adjectives have irregular comparatives.

| Base Form | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| bueno/a (good) | mejor (better) | el/la mejor (the best) |
| malo/a (bad) | peor (worse) | el/la peor (the worst) |
| grande (big) | mayor (bigger/older) | el/la mayor (the biggest/oldest) |
| pequeño/a (small) | menor (smaller/younger) | el/la menor (the smallest/youngest) |

**Examples:**
- Esta película es **mejor** que esa / This movie is better than that one
- Es el **peor** día de mi vida / It's the worst day of my life
- Mi hermano **mayor** / My older brother

## Study Tips

1. **Practice Gender and Number Agreement**: Always practice changing adjectives with nouns
2. **Pay Attention to Position**: Learn patterns for adjectives before/after nouns
3. **Memorize by Theme**: Group by emotions, personality, appearance, etc.
4. **Learn in Opposite Pairs**: Memorize pairs like grande-pequeño, alto-bajo
5. **Learn in Sentences**: Use adjectives in sentences rather than in isolation
6. **Apply to Real Life**: Describe objects around you with Spanish adjectives
7. **Color Practice**: Name the color of everything you see in Spanish
